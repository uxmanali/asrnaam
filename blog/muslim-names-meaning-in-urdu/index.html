How AsrNaam reads a name in Urdu

Urdu inherits nearly all of its naming vocabulary from Arabic. The Arabic root of a name carries its meaning; the Urdu spelling preserves the pronunciation with the addition of Persian-Urdu letters (پ Pa, چ Cha, ژ Zhe, گ Gaf) where the sound requires them.

AsrNaam gives you three things for every Urdu-facing family: the standard Urdu spelling (with correct nastaliq form), the meaning in plain Urdu prose, and the letter-by-letter reading that comes from the classical Islamic Ilm ul Huroof tradition. The Urdu name spelling guide explains why some spellings vary.

Choosing an Urdu name for your child

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud). For Urdu-speaking families, "good name" typically means:

  • Meaningful in Arabic. The root should carry a clear positive meaning; not just a fashionable-sounding syllable.
  • Pronounceable in Urdu. Both nastaliq spelling and rolling off the tongue in daily Urdu speech.
  • Not tied to fallen figures. Some names of tyrants (Firaun, Namrud) are avoided by tradition.
  • Rooted, not isolated. Names shared with prophets, companions, and the pious tie a child into that lineage.
