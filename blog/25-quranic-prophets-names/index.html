The 25 Quranic Prophets: names, meanings, and letter readings

AsrNaam Editorial. Updated 2026-07-02

The twenty-five prophets named directly in the Quran, from Adam to Muhammad, each with the Arabic spelling, the essential meaning, and the letter-by-letter Ilm ul Huroof reading that runs through the name. Read as a sequence, they trace the Islamic prophetic tradition's central names.

Note on the countThe Quran directly names twenty-five prophets. Muslim tradition holds that there were more (the hadith mentions 124,000 messengers), but this article covers the twenty-five whose names appear in the Quran itself, plus one, Uzair, whose prophet status is discussed. Each name links to the full AsrNaam page.

آدم Adam

From the Arabic *adama*, of the earth. The first man and prophet, from whom all humanity descends. The letter Alif opens him, origin itself, followed by Dal (the door of transition) and Meem (the maternal, water); a name that reads as origin-transition-return.

ادریس Idris

The instructor. Second prophet named. Traditionally identified with Enoch; scholar of writing and stars. The Ya at his centre is aspiration; the Ra is motion; the Seen is flowing; a name of movement through knowledge.

نوح Nuh

Long-living. The Prophet of the Flood. Nuh's ark is the vessel, matching the Nun (the inkwell, hidden treasure) at his name's opening. He waited nearly a thousand years to be believed.

ھود Hud

Prophet sent to the people of Ad. His name reads as the Ha-breath, the Waw of connection, and the Dal of the door; the messenger opening and closing a door.

صالح Salih

The righteous, the sound one. Prophet sent to Thamud. His name carries the Sad, the unbending upright, followed by Alif (origin) and Lam (authority) and Ha-breath; a name of integrity as posture.

ابراہیم Ibrahim

Father of nations, Friend of Allah (Khalilullah). Builder of the Kaaba with Ismail. Passed the test of sacrifice. His name is one of the longest of any prophet: origin (Alif), vessel (Ba), motion (Ra), origin returning (Alif), presence (Ha-light), aspiration (Ya), maternal (Meem); a founder whose lineage returns everything to source.

لوط Lut

Nephew of Ibrahim. Prophet sent to Sodom. Lam (authority), Waw (connection), Ta-heavy (manifestation); a name of speaking authority into a world that would not receive it.

اسماعیل Ismail

God hears. Son of Ibrahim by Hajar. The son who was to be sacrificed and was ransomed. Alif-Seen-Meem-Ain-Ya-Lam; origin flowing into maternal perception, aspiration, and authority. The letter Ain (perception) is the hinge.

اسحاق Ishaq

He laughs. Son of Ibrahim by Sarah. Prophet of the Israelites. Alif-Seen-Ha-breath-Alif-Qaf; origin, flow, enclosed breath, origin returning, and the axis of Qaf. A name of a promise fulfilled after long waiting.

یعقوب Yakub

The follower, the successor. Son of Ishaq, father of Yusuf. Also called Israel, he-who-strives. Ya-Ain-Qaf-Waw-Ba; aspiration, perception, axis, connection, containment. Father of the twelve tribes.

یوسف Yusuf

Prophet of beauty. Son of Yakub. Interpreter of dreams. Sold into slavery, rose to become vizier of Egypt. Ya-Waw-Seen-Fa; aspiration, connection, flow, opening. The letter Fa (opening) is where the story becomes his name.

شعیب Shuaib

Prophet of Madyan. Father-in-law of Musa in Sunni tradition. Sheen-Ain-Ya-Ba; radiance, perception, aspiration, vessel. The Sheen at his opening is the spreading light; he is a name of teaching-as-radiance.

ایوب Ayyub

The one of return. Prophet of patience. Tested with the loss of family, wealth, and health, and never cursed his Lord. Alif-Ya-Waw-Ba; origin, aspiration, connection, containment. The Alif that endures.

ذوالکفل Dhulkifl

The one of the fold, the one of the double portion. A Prophet of endurance. See also Dhul-Kifl in its longer form.

موسی Musa

Drawn from the water. Prophet of the Torah, of the ten plagues, of the parting of the sea, of Sinai. Meem-Waw-Seen-Ya; the maternal water is his opening, then connection, flow, and aspiration. Water is his name and his life.

ھارون Harun

Brother of Musa. The eloquent, the priestly. Ha-breath-Alif-Ra-Waw-Nun; breath, origin, motion, connection, and Nun the inkwell of the hidden. The elder brother who was made to speak so that Musa could act.

داوود Dawud

The beloved. King and prophet, father of Sulaiman. Slayer of Goliath. Author of the Zabur (Psalms). Dal-Alif-Waw-Waw-Dal; the door opens to origin, connects, connects again, and closes at the door. Beloved and bookended.

سلیمان Sulaiman

Peace-carrier. Son of Dawud. King of jinn, birds, and men. Wise judge. Seen-Lam-Ya-Meem-Alif-Nun; flow, authority, aspiration, maternal, origin, inkwell. The most complete kingdom, held in a single name.

الیاس Ilyas

Prophet Elias / Elijah. Prophet sent to reform his people from idolatry. Alif-Lam-Ya-Alif-Seen; origin, authority, aspiration, origin returning, flow.

الیسع Alyasa

Elisha, successor of Ilyas. A prophet after him, continuing his work.

یونس Yunus

Prophet of the whale. Sent to Nineveh. Ya-Waw-Nun-Seen; aspiration, connection, Nun (the inkwell, hidden), Seen (flow). His name literally contains a hidden interior, and his story is the Prophet inside the whale.

زکریا Zakariya

Father of Yahya. Guardian of Maryam. Prayed for a son in his old age and was answered. Zay-Kaf-Ra-Ya-Alif; ornament, sufficiency (kun), motion, aspiration, origin.

یحیی Yahya

Living, alive. John the Baptist. Son of Zakariya. Named directly by Allah before his birth ("We have not given this name to anyone before him", 19:7). Ya-Ha-breath-Ya-Ya; aspiration, breath, aspiration, aspiration. A name that is almost pure ascent.

عیسی Isa

Prophet Isa. Word of Allah, born to Maryam without a father. Ain-Ya-Seen-Ya; perception, aspiration, flow, aspiration. A name of pure perception.

مُحَمَّد Muhammad

The most praised. Seal of the Prophets. Meem-Ha-breath-Meem-Dal; the maternal, breath, the maternal returning, and the door. The most-mentioned name in Islamic memory.

What runs through all twenty-five

Reading the prophets' names as a sequence, some patterns stand out. Alif (origin) is the most common opening letter. Ha-breath is dense in the earlier prophets (Ibrahim, Ishaq, Musa's brother Harun) and Meem (maternal) is present at the extremes (Musa, Muhammad). Ya (aspiration) is the most frequent letter overall, appearing in nearly every name.

The tradition reads this as no accident. Prophethood in Islam is aspiration, presence, and origin returning; the letters of the prophetic names are the letters of that pattern.

Naming a child after a prophet

Naming a child after a prophet is one of the most encouraged practices in Islam. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Name your children after the prophets" (Abu Dawud). Among the most commonly given prophetic names today are:

  • Muhammad, the most given name in the world across four centuries
  • Ibrahim, the Friend of Allah
  • Yusuf, the Prophet of beauty
  • Ismail, God hears
  • Isa, born to Maryam
  • Musa, drawn from the water
  • Dawud, the beloved king
  • Sulaiman, the wise ruler
  • Yahya, named directly by Allah
  • Nuh, the long-lived one

Prophets, their wives, and their mothers

The Quran and Sunnah remember not only the prophets themselves but the women around them. AsrNaam has full pages for each:

  • Hawwa (Eve), wife of Adam
  • Sarah, wife of Ibrahim and mother of Ishaq
  • Hajar (Hagar), mother of Ismail
  • Asiya, wife of Pharaoh who raised Musa
  • Yukabid, mother of Musa
  • Maryam, mother of Isa
  • Khadijah, first wife of the Prophet Muhammad
  • Aisha, wife of the Prophet, Mother of Believers
  • Fatima, daughter of the Prophet
