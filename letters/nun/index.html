The Letter Itself

What Nun encodes

Nun's visual form is a vessel — like Meem but deeper, like a boat on water. نور (nur — light) begins with Nun. نفس (nafs — the self, the soul) begins with Nun. The letter governs what is preserved in depth: the light that exists in darkness, the self that persists through change.

Ilm ul Huroof

Ibn Arabi and the Science of Letters

Nun opens the Muqatta'at ن alone (Surah 68 — Al-Qalam, the Pen), which opens: "Nun. By the Pen and what they write." Classical scholars understood Nun as the primordial ink vessel — the container of the divine pen's writing. What is written is preserved. A name closing with Nun closes with the principle of preserved depth: the person leaves something written.

"Nun as a first letter governs the outward self — the primary mode through which the person meets the world and is met by it. Everything else in the name operates within the field this letter establishes."

In a Name

Nun as the first letter

Less commonly a first letter, Nun appears most significantly as a closing letter — ending names like Usman, Imran, Sulaiman, Hassan. A Nun-ending name means the life resolves toward what is preserved and written: the person leaves a record, a legacy, something that remains.

Quranic Resonance

Nun in the Quran

Nun opens ن والقلم (Nun, by the Pen — Surah 68). It is in نور (light), نبي (prophet), and نفس (soul). The preserved depth governs light, prophecy, and the self.
