Dictionary Meaning

What Bahlul means

The noble-generous — Bahlul Lodi was the Afghan founder of the Lodi dynasty of Delhi.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Bahlul

From Arabic bahlūl — "noble, generous; a man of laughter and wisdom." Bahlūl was also the name of a famous Abbasid-era wise fool of Kufa.

A name of generosity and ready wisdom. Bahlūl al-Majnūn was a celebrated Sufi-styled figure in Abbasid Baghdad.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Bahlul reveals

Read through the Science of Letters, Bahlul opens with Ba (ب), the letter of building, and resolves on Lam (ل), the letter of reaching. Between the two stand origin, awareness, reaching, origin, reaching. — a chord of interior qualities the name carries forward.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of the name Bahlul often carry the quality of building as the way they first meet the world — this is the work of the opening Ba (ب). The interior letters carry origin, awareness — a private current that gives the name its weight inside relationships and in work. And the name closes on Lam (ل) — the letter of reaching — so the bearer's life tends to resolve into that quality more than any other.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

Famous Bearers

Historical figures who carried the name Bahlul.

  • Bahlul Lodi (d. 1489 CE · Delhi) — Founder of the Lodi dynasty of Delhi; first Pashtun-Afghan sultan of Delhi.
