Dictionary Meaning

What Elma means

Elma is a Bosnian and Turkish-Muslim feminine name. In Turkish it carries the meaning of elma — apple — drawing on the symbolic richness of the apple tree in Anatolian and Sufi poetry, where it represents the heart of the lover, the moment of divine revelation, and earthly sweetness. In Bosnian Muslim usage, Elma is also read as a softened form of Arabic-origin names with the ʾ-l-m root (knowledge, learning) and stands as a sister-form to Selma and Belma. Common throughout Bosnia-Herzegovina, Kosovo, Albania, and the Bosniak diaspora since the Ottoman period; in Turkey, Elma is used both as a given name and as a poetic image. The simplicity of the two-syllable form (EL-ma) makes it easy to carry across European, Turkish, and Arab linguistic environments while remaining unmistakably a Muslim name of Balkan/Anatolian origin.

People named Elma tend to be open and self-possessed in how they meet the world. The repeated Alif at both ends suggests a person who lands things on a clear, singular note.

The Portrait

What Elma asks of its bearer

Elma opens with Alif — primary orientation toward being first. Inside the name, Lam brings an interior bound by love and attachment; Meem brings an interior of love and water. These are what the name does without ever announcing it. And Elma closes on Alif: everything resolves back to the source. The work of the name is to let apple, fair live through every letter — so that her life takes the same shape as her name.
