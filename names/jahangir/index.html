Dictionary Meaning

What Jahangir means

World-conqueror, holder of the world — from Persian جهانگیر (jahangir — jahan/world + gir/holder, conqueror). The one who holds the world in his grasp. Jahangir (1569–1627) was the fourth Mughal Emperor, son of Akbar the Great, whose reign produced some of the greatest works of Mughal art and architecture.

Despite his name suggesting conquest, Jahangir is remembered for his remarkable justice — he hung a golden chain of justice from his palace so that any subject could ring it and receive a hearing from the emperor directly.

Reading Jahangir through Ilm ul Huroof

What Jahangir asks of its bearer

People named Jahangir tend to have a quality of broad vision — they see and affect situations at a scale larger than themselves. The Jeem gathers comprehensively. The Nun gives depth. The Ra close resolves in mercy: their great reach is ultimately merciful.
