Dictionary Meaning

What Shabana means

Of the night, the nocturnal beauty — from Persian/Urdu shabana (of the night). In Persian and Urdu tradition the night is celebrated for its stillness, mystery, and beauty. Shabana Azmi is one of the greatest Indian film actresses — five-time National Award winner — who gave the name enormous South Asian recognition.

Pronunciationsha-BA-na  ·  sha·ba·na  ·  3 syllables

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Shabana reveals

Sheen of radiance opens, Ba of foundation, Alif of origin, Nun of depth, Alif. Night made into radiance.

People often notice a radiating quality, with their effect felt at the edges in those who carry this name.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run a felt presence — people pick up on them quickly. The Ba that runs through the name points to a steady, grounded inner posture that values structure — someone who builds durable relationships from a foundation of service. The closing Ha suggests a person who keeps life breathing forward, with few hard endings.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

Is Shabana mentioned in the Quran?
The night is honoured: 'By the night when it covers' (92:1). 'The night of Al-Qadr is better than a thousand months' (97:3). Shabana carries the beauty of the honoured night.
