Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Al-Masudi's letters draw from many elements at once, a full palette, not a single note. This is a name that asks its bearer to hold contradictions gracefully: warmth and steadiness, movement and depth. Running through the reading is Al-Ahad, the One, carried by a person as a singular, undivided presence. A person named Al-Masudi is many things at once, and gets to choose which shows up when.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

The nisba al-Masudi, from the name Masud (fortunate, blessed), the lineage marker of Abu al-Hasan Ali al-Masudi (d. 956 CE), the greatest Arab historian and geographer of the Islamic Golden Age, called the Herodotus of the Arabs.

Al-Masudi's Muruj al-Dhahab wa Maadin al-Jawhar (The Meadows of Gold and Mines of Gems) is the classical Arabic history of the world from creation to his own day, incorporating geography, ethnography, natural history, and political narrative. It shaped Arabic historiography for a millennium.

The name preserves the memory of a scholar whose curiosity spanned the known world of his tenth century.

Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Abū al-Ḥasan ʿAlī ibn al-Ḥusayn al-Masʿūdī (c. 896–956 CE · Baghdad / Cairo / Fustat). Al-Masʿūdī's Murūj al-Dhahab wa-Maʿādin al-Jawhar (Meadows of Gold and Mines of Gems) is the first universal history in Arabic to integrate geography, ethnography, and political narrative into a single work. He travelled to India, East Africa, China, and the Caspian region himself, and recorded customs, languages, and political histories of dozens of peoples, including the early Slavs, the Bulgars, and the kingdoms of the Indian Ocean trade.

About Al-Masudi

Who was Al-Masudi?
Al-Masudi (c. 896–956 CE) was The Herodotus of the Arabs. Abū al-Ḥasan ʿAlī ibn al-Ḥusayn al-Masʿūdī. Active in Baghdad / Cairo / Fustat, field: Historiography, geography.
What does the name Al-Masudi mean?
Al-Masudi means: From Masʿūd, fortunate, blessed; the lineage marker of a happy line.
Is Al-Masudi an Islamic name?
Yes. Al-Masudi is a recognised Arabic name of significant historical weight in the Muslim world, borne by The Herodotus of the Arabs.
