What Al-Masudi means

Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Al-Masudi.

  • Abū al-Ḥasan ʿAlī ibn al-Ḥusayn al-Masʿūdī (c. 896–956 CE · Baghdad / Cairo / Fustat). Al-Masʿūdī's Murūj al-Dhahab wa-Maʿādin al-Jawhar (Meadows of Gold and Mines of Gems) is the first universal history in Arabic to integrate geography, ethnography, and political narrative into a single work. He travelled to India, East Africa, China, and the Caspian region himself, and recorded customs, languages, and political histories of dozens of peoples, including the early Slavs, the Bulgars, and the kingdoms of the Indian Ocean trade.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Al-Masudi reveals

Al-Masʿūdī opens with Alif (ا), origin, and closes on Ya (ي), reaching. The interior carries Sīn (س) of horizon and Dāl (د) of the door: a name shaped to cross thresholds.

Legacy

What Al-Masudi left behind

Murūj al-Dhahab was used by Ibn Khaldūn, by Ottoman court historians, and by every later Arabic geographer. Al-Masʿūdī's mention of the Vikings (al-majūs) on the Volga is one of the earliest external accounts of Norse civilisation. He pioneered a non-annalistic, thematic style of historical writing.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
