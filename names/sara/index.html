Dictionary Meaning

What Sara means

Sāra (سارة) is the Arabic form of the Hebrew Sarah שָׂרָה — 'noblewoman, princess' — the wife of the prophet Ibrāhīm and the mother of the prophet Isḥāq. Although she is not named directly in the Qurʾān, the classical Islamic exegetical tradition (al-Ṭabarī, Ibn Kathīr, qiṣaṣ al-anbiyāʾ) preserves her story in detail: her marriage to Ibrāhīm, their migration to Egypt, the encounter with the Egyptian pharaoh that ended in her vindication, the giving of Hagar to Ibrāhīm as a co-wife after Sara's long childlessness, and finally the angelic announcement of her own miraculous pregnancy with Isḥāq in her advanced age (Qurʾān 11:71–73).

The name has independent etymological resonance in Persian, where sara means 'pure, simple, unmixed' — a meaning that converges with the Hebrew nobility-meaning to produce a name with a quietly elevated register across the Persianate world. Sara is among the most common feminine names across Iran, Pakistan, the Arab world, and Turkey. Notable bearers include Sara Aboubakr, the Egyptian-Sudanese novelist; Sara Shahi, the Iranian-American actress; and the Saudi football coach Sara al-Mehairy.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Sara

س-ر-و — from s-r-w, to be noble; or Hebrew sarah, princess. The wife of Sayyiduna Ibrāhīm (AS) and mother of Sayyiduna Isḥāq (AS).
