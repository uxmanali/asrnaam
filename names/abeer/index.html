Dictionary Meaning

What Abeer means

From the Arabic ʿabīr (عبير) — perfume, a fragrance specifically of saffron mixed with other aromatics, in classical Arabian usage the perfume worn at celebrations. The lexicographers gloss ʿabīr as the powdered fragrant mixture; in modern Arabic it has come to mean perfume more generally. The image is of fragrance carried on the air — invisible but unmistakable presence.

A widely-used girls' name across the Gulf, the Levant, and Egypt. Bearer notes: Abeer Issa, Saudi-Egyptian-American business executive; Abeer Allam, Saudi-American journalist. Pair-names in Saudi-Gulf families: Lulu, Maha, Reem, Hessa.

Variants: Abeer (Saudi-Gulf preferred), Abeera, Abiri, Abir (Egyptian). Same fragrance-family: Misk (musk), Anbar (amber), Yasmin (jasmine), Ward (rose). Compound: Abeer-Mariam, Abeer-Yasmine. The masculine ʿAbīr is rare.
