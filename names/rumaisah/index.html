Dictionary Meaning

What Rumaisah means

The one with beautiful eyes — from root ر-م-ص (ramasa — to have a glistening, beautiful eye). رميصاء (Rumaisah — the one with the beautiful glistening eyes). This is a nickname given to Umm Sulaym bint Milhan (RA) — one of the most remarkable companions of the Prophet ﷺ. Her real name was Ghumaydah or Sahla, but she was known as Rumaisah.

Umm Sulaym (RA) was called 'one of the virgins of paradise' by the Prophet ﷺ. When her husband Abu Talha proposed to her, she said her mahr (dowry) would be his conversion to Islam — and he accepted. She carried a dagger at Uhud. She showed extraordinary patience at the death of her son. She is one of the most celebrated women companions.

Rumaisah is trending in Pakistan and the Arab world in 2026.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Rumaisah

ر-م-ص — variant of Rumaysāʾ. Umm Sulaym Rumaysāʾ bint Milḥān (RA) was a major Companion, mother of Anas ibn Mālik.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Rumaisah reveals

Ra of mercy opens, Waw of connection, Meem of love, Alif of origin, Ya of reaching, Sin of secret, Ha of presence. A Sahabiya's name made present.

People often notice a fluid warmth and adaptiveness that shifts with the room in those who carry this name.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run energetic and forward-moving. The Meem that runs through the name points to a deep capacity for love and a strong tie to family and inner faith — someone who tends to become the emotional centre of the people around them. The closing Hamza suggests a person who lands clearly and definitively.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

The Portrait

What Rumaisah asks of its bearer

People named Rumaisah tend to have a quality of beautiful, sincere perception — their eyes see truly and their gaze is remembered. The Ra moves with mercy. The Meem holds in love. The Sad gives sincere truth. The Hamza begins strongly.
