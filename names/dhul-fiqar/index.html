Dictionary Meaning

What Dhulfiqar means

The cleaver of vertebrae, the spine-splitter — from ذو (dhu — possessor of) + الفقار (al-fiqar — vertebrae, the backbone). Dhul Fiqar is the legendary double-bladed sword that the Prophet ﷺ gave to Ali ibn Abi Talib (RA). It became the most famous sword in Islamic history — a symbol of righteous courage and the sword of the person who is both the lion of God and the door of the Prophet's knowledge.

The famous saying 'La fata illa Ali, la sayf illa Dhulfiqar' — 'There is no young man like Ali, there is no sword like Dhulfiqar' — was reputedly called from the heavens at the Battle of Uhud. The name is used in Muslim communities (especially in Iran and Shia communities) as a name of power and honour.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Dhulfiqar

ف-ق-ر — Variant spelling of Dhū al-Faqār. From the root f-q-r (vertebra) — the sword of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, given by the Prophet ﷺ after the battle of Uhud. The blade is the standing symbol of ʿAlid courage.

The Letters

Reading Dhulfiqar through Ilm ul Huroof

Every letter in Dhulfiqar carries a role. Dhal opens it outward; the letters between hold the interior; the last letter sets how things land.

Dhulfiqar opens with Dhal (ذ) — the essence, the self, the remembering quality. Then Waw (و) — connection. Then Alif (ا) — origin. Then Lam (ل) — divine authority. Then Fa (ف) — subtle opening. Then Qaf (ق) — the divine axis. Then Alif (ا) — return. Then Ra (ر) — mercy in motion. Essence — connection — origin — divine authority — opening — the absolute pole — return — mercy. The sword that cleaves: possessing essence, connecting, rooted in origin, authoritative, opening the way, grounded in the absolute, returning, and moving with mercy.

The Portrait

What Dhulfiqar asks of its bearer

People named Dhulfiqar tend to have a quality of decisive clarity — they cut through confusion. The Dhal gives them a quality of essential self: they know who they are. The Qaf grounds them in the absolute. The Ra close moves with mercy: the legendary sword's owner was the man of mercy and love. True decisiveness always resolves in mercy.

A Notable Bearer

Dhulfiqar — the legendary double-bladed sword given by the Prophet ﷺ to Ali ibn Abi Talib (RA). 'There is no young man like Ali, there is no sword like Dhulfiqar.' It became the symbol of righteous courage and the emblem of the Shia tradition.
