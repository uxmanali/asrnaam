What the name Bushraa means, what its letters say, and what it asks of the person who carries it.

Dictionary Meaning

What Bushraa means

Glad tidings, the best of good news — from root ب-ش-ر (bashshara — to give glad tidings, to announce good news). بشرى (Bushra — glad tidings, the announcement of great good news). The Quran uses this word in some of its most celebrated verses: 'Lahum al-bushra fi al-hayat al-dunya wa fi al-akhirah — For them are glad tidings in the worldly life and in the Hereafter' (10:64). And the angel's announcement to Maryam (AS): 'O Maryam, indeed Allah gives you glad tidings (yubashiruki) of a word from Him' (3:45).

Bushraa/Bushra names the woman who is herself the glad tidings — whose arrival in people's lives is the good news they were waiting for. The double 'a' spelling (Bushraa) is the more Arabic-faithful transliteration capturing distinct search volume.

The Letters

Reading Bushraa through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

بBaFirst · Outward SelfشSheenInteriorرRaInteriorىYaLast · Resolution

Bushraa opens with Ba (ب) — the vessel. Then Sheen (ش) — solar, radiant witness. Then Ra (ر) — perpetual mercy. Then Ya at rest (ى) — completed reaching. Vessel — solar radiance — perpetual mercy — arrived. The glad tidings: a vessel for the best news, radiating like the sun, moving with perpetual mercy, arriving complete. Good news arrives complete.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Bushraa asks of its bearer

People named Bushraa tend to be experienced by others as good news — their arrival changes the atmosphere toward the positive. The Ba makes them a vessel: they carry the good news without distorting it. The Sheen radiates. The Ra moves with perpetual mercy. The Ya at rest arrives complete: she arrives and the news is fully given.

A Notable Bearer

'For them are glad tidings (al-bushra) in the worldly life and in the Hereafter' — Quran 10:64. 'O Maryam, indeed Allah gives you glad tidings of a word from Him' — Quran 3:45. Bushraa is the Quranic word for the greatest good news.

About the name Bushraa

What does the name Bushraa mean?
Bushraa means: Glad tidings — the Quranic word for the greatest of good news. From root ب-ش-ر (glad tidings). Directly from Quran 10:64: 'For them are glad tidings in this life and the Hereafter.' The angel gave Maryam (AS) glad tidings. A Quranic name of the highest quality.
Is Bushraa mentioned in the Quran?
Yes — bushra appears directly in the Quran. 'For them are glad tidings (al-bushra) in the worldly life and in the Hereafter' (10:64). The angel gave Maryam (AS) glad tidings (yubashiruki) of Eisa (3:45). The Prophet ﷺ is described as a 'giver of glad tidings (bashiran)' (2:119). A directly Quranic name.
What does Bushraa mean in Urdu?

Is Bushraa a good Islamic name?
Yes, Bushraa is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: Glad tidings — the Quranic word for the greatest of good news. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
Is Bushraa a girl or boy name?
Bushraa is traditionally a Muslim girl name meaning Glad tidings — the Quranic word for the greatest of good news.
What is the spiritual meaning of Bushraa in Ilm ul Huroof?
Ba (ب) is the vessel, Sheen (ش) radiates like the sun, Ra (ر) moves with perpetual mercy, Ya (ى) arrives complete. Glad tidings: holding, radiant, merciful, complete — good news that arrives fully.
