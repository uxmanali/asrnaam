Dictionary Meaning

What Iltutmish means

Holder of the realm — the third sultan of the Mamluk dynasty of Delhi, true consolidator of the Delhi Sultanate.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Iltutmish

Old Turkic il (state, kingdom) + tutmish (one who has held/seized) — "he who has held the realm."

A Turkic ghulām name marking enthronement: the one whose hand has closed on the kingdom.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Iltutmish reveals

Read through the Science of Letters, Iltutmish opens with Alif (ا), the letter of origin, and resolves on Meem (م), the letter of love. Between the two stand reaching, completion, origin, completion, love. — a chord of interior qualities the name carries forward.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of the name Iltutmish often carry the quality of origin as the way they first meet the world — this is the work of the opening Alif (ا). The interior letters carry reaching, completion — a private current that gives the name its weight inside relationships and in work. And the name closes on Meem (م) — the letter of love — so the bearer's life tends to resolve into that quality more than any other.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

Famous Bearers

Historical figures who carried the name Iltutmish.

  • Shams-ud-din Iltutmish (d. 1236 CE · Delhi) — Third sultan of the Mamluk dynasty of Delhi; true consolidator of the Delhi Sultanate; father of Razia Sultan.
