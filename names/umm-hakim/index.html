About the name

Umm Hakim is an Arabic name, drawn from the classical Arabic lexicographic tradition (Lisan al-Arab, al-Sihah, Taj al-Arus). The name carries the meaning: The mother of Hakim. Wife of Ikrimah ibn Abi Jahl; witness at Yarmouk.

Every letter of Umm Hakim in the Arabic script has been read in the classical tradition of Ilm ul Huroof as an ontological principle: the letters do not merely spell the sound of the name, they carry the qualities the tradition ascribes to it. See the letter reading above.

In Islam, the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "You will be called on the Day of Resurrection by your names and the names of your fathers, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud). A well-chosen name is a lifelong invocation of the meaning it carries.
