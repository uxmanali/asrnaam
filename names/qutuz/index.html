Dictionary Meaning

What Qutuz means

A Turkic war-name — Sayf al-Dīn Quṭuz, third Mamluk sultan of Egypt and victor at the Battle of ʿAyn Jālūt.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Qutuz

Old Turkic personal name, sometimes glossed "fierce dog." His regnal title was al-Muẓaffar — "the Victorious."

A Kipchak Mamluk court-name carried by the sultan who broke the Mongol invasion in 1260.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Qutuz reveals

Read through the Science of Letters, Qutuz opens with Qaf (ق), the letter of standing, and resolves on Zayn (ز), the letter of adornment. Between the two stand origin, completion, origin, adornment. — a chord of interior qualities the name carries forward.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of the name Qutuz often carry the quality of standing as the way they first meet the world — this is the work of the opening Qaf (ق). The interior letters carry origin, completion — a private current that gives the name its weight inside relationships and in work. And the name closes on Zayn (ز) — the letter of adornment — so the bearer's life tends to resolve into that quality more than any other.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

Famous Bearers

Historical figures who carried the name Qutuz.

  • Sayf al-Din Qutuz (d. 1260 CE · Cairo) — Third Mamluk sultan of Egypt; victor at the Battle of ʿAyn Jālūt — the first major defeat of the Mongol army.
