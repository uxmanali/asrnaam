Dictionary Meaning

What Sajid means

One who prostrates, the worshipper — from root س-ج-د (sajada — to prostrate, to bow down in worship). ساجد (sajid — the one who prostrates, the one in a state of sujud). Sujud (prostration) is the most intimate position in Islamic prayer — the moment when the forehead touches the earth, the highest point of the body brought to the lowest, in the ultimate act of humility before the divine.

The Quran says: 'Prostrate and draw near' (96:19). Sajid names the man whose defining quality is this continuous prostration — not merely in the physical prayer but in the orientation of his entire life toward the divine.

The Letters

Reading Sajid through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

سSinFirst · Outward SelfاAlifInteriorجJeemInteriorدDalLast · Resolution

Sajid opens with Sin (س) — flowing expansion. Then Alif (ا) — origin. Then Jeem (ج) — comprehensive beauty. Then Dal (د) — the returning door. Flowing expansion — origin — comprehensive beauty — the returning door. The prostrating one: flowing outward in worship, rooted in origin, gathering comprehensive beauty in the act of prostration, and always returning through the door. Prostration is the ultimate returning door.

The Portrait

What Sajid asks of its bearer

People named Sajid tend to have a quality of genuine, continuous devotion — their life is oriented toward the divine in a way others can feel. The Sin gives them flowing, expansive worship. The Alif grounds in origin. The Jeem gathers comprehensive beauty in the act of worship. The Dal close keeps returning: the sajid is defined by return to the earth, to the divine.

A Notable Bearer

'Prostrate and draw near' — Quran 96:19. The last verse revealed in Makkah commanded this. Sajid names the man who has made his entire life a response to this command.
