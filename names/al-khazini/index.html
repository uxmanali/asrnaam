Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Al-Khazini.

  • Abū al-Fatḥ ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Khāzinī (fl. c. 1115 – c. 1130 CE · Merv). Al-Khāzinī's Kitāb Mīzān al-Ḥikma (Book of the Balance of Wisdom) is the most comprehensive medieval treatise on the hydrostatic balance. He compiled accurate specific-gravity values for many substances, refined the theory of weighing, discussed centres of gravity, and explored the relation of weight to depth in air and water. He also produced the Sanjarī Zīj, an astronomical table for sultan Sanjar, and wrote on calendrical computation.

Legacy

What Al-Khazini left behind

The Mīzān remains the principal source for understanding pre-modern Islamic mechanics and is consulted today by historians of physics for evidence of how the discipline operated between Archimedes and the European seventeenth century.
