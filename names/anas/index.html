Dictionary Meaning

What Anas means

From the Arabic uns (أنس) — intimate familiarity, the sense of ease in the company of a beloved friend. The verb anisa means 'to find oneself at home, to be intimately at peace'. The same root yields insān (human being — the one who carries this capacity for intimate connection with Allah and other humans). The Sufi term mahabbah-uns is the lover's intimate familiarity with the Beloved.

The most weight-bearing companion-bearer is Anas ibn Mālik (d. 712 CE) — the servant of the Prophet ﷺ from the age of ten, who narrated more than 2,200 hadith and lived to be over 100. The Prophet prayed for him: 'O Allah, multiply his wealth and his children, and bless what You have given him' (Bukhari). Anas became one of the wealthiest of the Companions.

One of the most-used boys' names across the Arab world, Pakistan, and Turkey. Variants: Anas (standard), Enes (Turkish), Anass (Maghrebi-French), Anees (Pakistani — slightly different word). Pair-names: Bilal, Salman, Hamza, Saad.

Arabic Root Meaning

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Anas reveals

Alif origin, Nun depth, Sin expansion. A friendliness that begins at origin, roots deep, and expands outward — true companionship.

People often notice a quiet refusal to be folded into the crowd in those who carry this name.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run open and self-possessed in how they meet the world. The Nun that runs through the name points to an inwardness that watches and contemplates — someone who is the friend who notices, who remembers the small things. The closing Sin suggests a person who settles into support — resolves into service.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.
