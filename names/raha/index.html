Dictionary Meaning

What Raha means

Rest, peace, ease, comfort — from root ر-و-ح (raha — rest, ease, the relief that comes after difficulty). راحة (Raha — rest, peace, ease). The Quran promises this: 'Fa rawh wa rayhaan wa jannat na'eem — Then rest (rawh) and fragrance and a garden of bliss' (56:89) — for those who are near to Allah. And: 'La yukallif Allah nafsan illa wus'aha — Allah does not burden a soul beyond what it can bear' (2:286) — after every burden comes raha.

Raha is in the top 39 most searched Muslim baby names on QuranicNames.com in 2026 (18%), treasured across the Arab world and diaspora for its simple, beautiful meaning of divine rest and peace.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Raha

ر-ح-ا — from r-ḥ, ease, rest. Raḥā can be the millstone (one that grinds for sustenance) or rāḥa, the ease — the rest of the soul.

The Letters

Reading Raha through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

رRaFirst · Outward SelfmercyاAlifInteriororiginحHaInteriorlife-breathةTaLast · Resolutionmanifestation

Raha: Ra (ر) — perpetual mercy. Alif (ا) — origin. Ha (ح) — the breath of life, divine intimacy. Ha (ه) — divine presence. Perpetual mercy — origin — divine breath of life — divine presence. The rest: moving with perpetual mercy, rooted in origin, breathing divine intimacy, breathing divine presence. True rest breathes the divine — twice.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Raha asks of its bearer

People named Raha tend to bring a quality of genuine rest to those around them — in their presence, others relax. The Ra moves with perpetual mercy. The double Ha breathes divine intimacy then divine presence: her rest is doubly divine.

A Notable Bearer

'Then rest (rawh) and fragrance and a garden of bliss' — Quran 56:89, the promise for those near to Allah. Raha names the woman who carries and gives this quality of divine rest.
