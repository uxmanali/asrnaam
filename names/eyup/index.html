Dictionary Meaning

What Eyüp means

Job — Turkish form of أيوب (Ayyub — the Prophet Job). Ayyub (AS) is one of the great prophets of Islam, celebrated above all for his extraordinary patience under affliction. He suffered the loss of wealth, children, and health — yet his faith never wavered. The Quran says: 'And remember Our servant Ayyub, when he called to his Lord: Adversity has touched me, and You are the Most Merciful of the merciful' (21:83).

Allah restored everything — his health, his family, his wealth — doubled. The Prophet ﷺ said Ayyub was afflicted for 18 years and then Allah cured him. His name became the synonym for patience itself: 'sabr Ayyub' (the patience of Job) is the standard by which all patience is measured in Islamic culture. Eyüp Sultan mosque in Istanbul is built over his tomb — one of the most sacred sites in Turkey.

The Letters

Reading Eyüp through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior life. The final letter governs how things resolve. For Turkish, Persian and Urdu names, the reading draws on the nearest Arabic letter equivalents.

اAlifFirst · Outward SelfيYaInteriorوWawInteriorبBaLast · Resolution

Eyüp opens with Alif (ا) — origin. Then Ya (ي) — the reaching hand. Then Waw (و) — connection, the divine conjunction. Then Ba (ب) — the vessel. Origin (Alif) — reaching (Ya) — connection (Waw) — the vessel (Ba). The patient prophet: rooted in origin, reaching always toward Allah, connected through the divine conjunction, becoming a vessel for what is given. The vessel receives the affliction and the restoration equally.

The Portrait

What Eyüp asks of its bearer

People named Eyüp tend to have a quality of deep endurance — they can hold more than others without breaking. The Alif grounds them in origin. The Ya reaches always toward what is beyond the immediate difficulty. The Waw connects them to the divine: their endurance is not merely stoic but connected. The Ba makes them a vessel: they receive both the pain and the restoration with equal openness.

A Notable Bearer

Ayyub (AS) — the Prophet of patience, whose endurance under affliction became the eternal measure of perseverance in Islam. 'Sabr Ayyub' — the patience of Job — is invoked whenever someone endures great hardship. Eyüp Sultan mosque in Istanbul marks his burial place and has been a site of Islamic visitation for 600 years.
