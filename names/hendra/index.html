The Letters

Reading Hendra through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

حHaFirst · Outward Selflife-breathنNunInteriordepthدDalInteriorreturnرRaInteriormercyاAlifLast · Resolutionorigin

Ha (breath of life) — Nun (depth) — Dal (returning door) — Ra (perpetual mercy) — Alif (return). Champion: breathing life, deep, returning, merciful, returning again.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi

The Portrait

What Hendra asks of its bearer

The reading of Hendra begins with Ha (soft): an outward presence that breathes. At the interior, Nun brings an interior of preserved depth; Dal brings an interior of return. These are the inward currents the name carries. The closing letter is Alif: everything resolves back to the source. Read together, the letters ask him to embody champion as a lived structure, not merely a definition.
