Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Mālik ibn Anas ibn Mālik al-Aṣbaḥī (c. 711–795 CE · Medina). Imām Mālik ibn Anas is the eponymous founder of the Mālikī madhhab, the second of the four classical Sunni schools by chronology and the dominant school across North Africa, West Africa, and Upper Egypt. His al-Muwaṭṭaʾ, the well-trodden path, is the earliest extant compendium of Islamic law and hadith, weaving together approximately seven hundred narrations from the Prophet ﷺ and the Companions with statements of the early Medinan jurists. His distinctive methodological principle was ʿamal ahl al-Madīna, the living practice of the people of Medina, as a source of law.

Legacy

What Malik ibn Anas left behind

The Mālikī madhhab predominates in Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, Libya, much of West Africa (Mauritania, Senegal, Mali, Nigeria), and Upper Egypt, with significant presence in the Gulf, Sudan, and Kuwait. Al-Muwaṭṭaʾ is one of the foundational texts of Islamic legal literature, taught continuously since its composition and serving as a benchmark for the integration of hadith and juristic reasoning.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
