Dictionary Meaning

What Mehnaz means

Moon-like grace — from Persian مهناز (Mehnaz — mah/moon + naz/graceful pride = the graceful one like the moon). The name combines the lunar beauty (mah — moon) with naz (graceful, coquettish elegance) — the woman whose grace is as soft and beautiful as the moon.

Mehnaz is widely used across Pakistan, India, and the broader Persian-speaking Muslim world. It is one of the classic compound names of the Urdu-Persian naming tradition, combining two of the most beloved poetic images — the moon and graceful elegance.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Mehnaz

Persian compound — māh (moon) + nāz (pride, grace). 'Pride of the moon' or 'graceful as moonlight'.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Mehnaz reveals

Meem of love opens, Ha of presence, Nun of depth, Alif of origin, Zayn of subtle radiance. Moonlight made into pride.

How This Shows Up in Life

Personality & relational pattern

People named Mehnaz tend to be soft and loving, instinctively familial in their bearing. The Ha that runs through the name points to an open, breath-like quality alive in conversation and feeling — someone who energises the people around them. The closing Zayn suggests a person who concludes things on a note of brightness.

An interpretive reading drawn from the letters of the name — for reflection, not prediction.

What does the name Mehnaz mean?

Is Mehnaz mentioned in the Quran?
The moon (qamar) appears 27 times in the Quran. 'He made the moon a light (nuran)' (10:5). The graceful lunar quality of Mehnaz reflects this Quranic lunar light combined with grace.
