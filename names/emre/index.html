Dictionary Meaning

What Emre means

Love, devotion — from Turkic emre (affection, devotion). Named after Yunus Emre — the 13th century Turkish Sufi poet whose poetry of divine love is one of the greatest literary achievements in Turkish Islam. Emre names the man of loving devotion quality. Top Turkish boys name.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Emre

أ-م-ر — Turkish emre — friend, companion, lover in mystical poetry. The form was fixed by Yunus Emre (d. c. 1320), the great Anatolian Sufi poet whose vernacular Turkish verse founded the Alevi-Bektashi devotional canon. The Arabic root a-m-r (command) is the etymological source.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Emre reveals

Alif of origin opens, Meem of love holds the heart, Ra carries gentle motion, and final Ta-marbuta seals the form complete. The letters trace love as something opened, held, moved, and finished.

How This Shows Up in Life

Personality & relational pattern

People named Emre tend to be open and self-possessed in how they meet the world. The Meem that runs through the name points to a deep capacity for love and a strong tie to family and inner faith — someone who tends to become the emotional centre of the people around them. The closing Ta suggests a person who completes things cleanly, with few loose ends.

An interpretive reading drawn from the letters of the name — for reflection, not prediction.

The Portrait

What Emre asks of its bearer

What carries Emre into the world is Ain: an outward self that sees deeply. Inside the name, Meem brings an interior of love and water; Ra brings an interior of repetition and momentum. These are the hidden weathers of the name. And Emre closes on Alif: everything resolves back to the source. Read together, the letters ask him to embody love as a lived structure, not merely a definition.
