Dictionary Meaning

What Amin means

Trustworthy, faithful, the custodian, the one in whom trust is placed — from root أ-م-ن (amina — to be safe, to be trusted, to believe). The root gives أمانة (amana — trust, the sacred trust), إيمان (iman — faith), أمان (aman — safety, security), and آمين (amin — the prayer seal: 'let it be so').

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was called Al-Amin — the Trustworthy — by the people of Makkah even before his prophethood, because of his known honesty and reliability. To name a child Amin is to name them after the Prophet's pre-revelation title.

The Letters

Reading Amin through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self — the primary mode through which the person meets the world. The middle letters govern the interior life. The final letter governs how things resolve.

اAlifFirst · Outward Self مMeemInterior يYaInterior نNunLast · Resolution

Amin opens with Alif — origin. Then Meem — love, depth, the sustaining. Then Ya — the reaching hand, active will. Then Nun — the primordial depth. The arc: from origin (Alif) — through love and depth (Meem) — reaching actively (Ya) — held in the primordial (Nun). Trust is built in this sequence: from the first principle, through genuine love, by active reaching, held in something deeper than the occasion.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi

The Portrait

What Amin asks of its bearer

People named Amin often become the natural keepers of others' secrets, confidence and valuables — not because they seek this role but because others instinctively know they can be trusted. The Alif grounds them in original principle. The Meem gives them love as the basis of their trustworthiness — they keep faith because they care, not because of duty alone. The Nun close gives them depth: what they hold, they hold well.

A Notable Bearer

Al-Amin — the Trustworthy — was the title given to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ by the Quraysh before revelation came, attesting to his honesty and integrity.
