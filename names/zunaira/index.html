Dictionary Meaning

What Zunaira means

Flowers of paradise — Zunaira was one of the early Muslim women who was tortured for her faith. She was the companion of the Prophet ﷺ, a slave who accepted Islam and was severely persecuted by Abu Jahl. Despite losing her eyesight under torture, she never recanted. When she miraculously regained her sight, Abu Jahl attributed it to magic — she said it was the mercy of Allah.

Zunaira is one of the top 5 trending Muslim girl names in Pakistan in 2025-2026. It carries the quality of the woman of steadfast faith — who holds to what she believes even under the severest trial, and whose faith is rewarded with divine mercy.

The Letters

Reading Zunaira through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

زZainFirst · Outward SelfنNunInteriorيYaInteriorرRaInteriorهHaLast · Resolution

Zunaira opens with Zain (ز) — adornment. Then Nun (ن) — primordial depth. Then Ya (ي) — reaching. Then Ra (ر) — perpetual mercy. Then Ha (ه) — divine presence. Adornment — primordial depth — reaching — perpetual mercy — divine presence. The faithful companion: adorning her faith with beauty, drawing from primordial depth, reaching toward what she believes, moving with perpetual mercy, breathing divine presence. Her faith adorns even what persecutes it.

The Portrait

What Zunaira asks of its bearer

People named Zunaira tend to have a quality of unwavering, adorned faith — they hold to what they believe and their faith makes everything more beautiful. The Zain adorns. The Nun holds in depth: their faith is very deep. The Ra moves with perpetual mercy. The Ha breathes divine presence.

A Notable Bearer

Zunaira bint Khabbab (RA) — one of the earliest Muslims who bore severe torture for her faith. She lost her sight under persecution but regained it through divine mercy. When Abu Jahl called it magic, she answered: 'No — it is the mercy of Allah.' A model of unwavering faith.

Zunaira bint Khabbab is not mentioned by name in the Quran, but her story is part of Islamic historical tradition. The early Muslim martyrs and steadfast believers are celebrated throughout the Quran: 'And the forerunners — the first of the Muhajirun and the Ansar — Allah is pleased with them and they are pleased with Him' (9:100). Zunaira was among these first believers.
