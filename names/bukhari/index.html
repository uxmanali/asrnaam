Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Bukhari.

  • Abū ʿAbd Allāh Muḥammad ibn Ismāʿīl al-Bukhārī (810–870 CE · Bukhara (Transoxania) / Nishapur / Khartank). Imām al-Bukhārī compiled al-Jāmiʿ al-Ṣaḥīḥ, known as Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, the most authoritative collection of prophetic hadith in Sunni Islam. He is reported to have sifted some 600,000 transmitted reports to extract approximately 7,275 hadiths (with repetition) that met his strict criteria of authenticity (al-Ṣaḥīḥ). The work is organised by legal and devotional topic across some ninety-seven chapters and remains, after the Qur'ān, the most cited text in Sunni religious literature.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Bukhari reveals

Bukhari opens with the vessel (Bā) and threads through the hidden (Khā), a name whose work was to contain and to verify what the senses cannot directly witness. The resolution in Yā is the receptive form: the hadith that the bearer becomes, in the end, the vehicle for.

Legacy

What Bukhari left behind

Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī is considered by Sunni scholarly consensus the most authentic book after the Qur'ān itself. His methodological standards, particularly the requirements of meeting (liqāʾ) and reliable transmission across an unbroken chain, became the benchmark for hadith verification in subsequent centuries.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
