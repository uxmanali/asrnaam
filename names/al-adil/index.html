Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

ع-د-ل

Definite-article ʿādil, "the just", from the root ʿ-d-l (to be just, to balance).

ع-د-ل, justice, balance; same root as ʿadl (justice) and al-ʿAdl, one of the divine attributes.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Water pools in Al-Adil, the element of mercy, feeling, and receptive depth. The letters ask their bearer to hold others without hardening, to be a well and not a wall. Anchoring this reading is Al-Ahad, the One, calling the person toward a singular, undivided presence. A person named Al-Adil holds others without asking them to be smaller.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

From the Arabic root ع-د-ل (to be just, to weigh evenly), al-Adil is the classical Arab title: the just, the impartial. Al-Adl (the Just) is one of the divine names of Allah in the classical enumeration.

The name is honoured in Sayf al-Din al-Adil (r. 1200 to 1218), brother of Saladin and second Ayyubid sultan of Egypt and Syria. He consolidated the Ayyubid state after Saladin's death and negotiated the peace with the Third Crusade that preserved Muslim rule of Jerusalem.

The name asks that a son grow into the justice that others receive as their birthright.
