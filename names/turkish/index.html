Turkish Muslim Names, Meaning, Arabic Form & Ilm ul Huroof

Modern and Ottoman-era Turkish names, including the Turkified forms of classical Arabic names and indigenous Turkic names with Islamic resonance.

59 names

Turkish Muslim naming sits at the meeting point of two streams. From the Arabic side come the names of the Prophet ﷺ, his Companions, and the Quranic vocabulary, re-spelled across centuries of Ottoman Turkish to fit the language's phonology: Muhammad becomes Mehmet, Aḥmad becomes Ahmet, ʿUmar becomes Ömer, ʿUthmān becomes Osman, Ḥusayn becomes Hüseyin. From the Turkic side come names of older Anatolian, Oghuz, and Central Asian provenance, Deniz (the sea), Yıldız (a star), Volkan (volcano), Kerem (generosity).

The Turkic forms are not transliterations. They are settled, canonical forms in their own right, used continuously for centuries in poetry, in the Ottoman registers, in modern Turkish, and across the Turkic-speaking world from the Balkans through Central Asia. Each entry below carries the Arabic spelling (where applicable), the standard romanisation, and the meaning the name carries inside the Turkish-Muslim tradition.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are popular Turkish Muslim names?

Popular Turkish Muslim names include Mehmet (Muhammad), Ahmet (Ahmad), Ömer (Umar), Mustafa, Hüseyin (Husayn), Ayşe (Aisha), Fatma (Fatima), Zeynep (Zaynab), Emre, Deniz, and Kerem. Many are Turkified forms of classical Arabic names that have been used continuously in Ottoman and modern Turkish since the 14th century.

Why are Turkish names different from Arabic names?

Turkish naming has been settling Arabic names into Turkish phonology since the Ottoman era, so Muhammad becomes Mehmet, Aḥmad becomes Ahmet, ʿUthmān becomes Osman. These are not transliterations; they are canonical Turkish forms in their own right, used continuously in Ottoman registers, modern Turkish, and across the broader Turkic world.

What is the most common Turkish boy name?

Mehmet (the Turkish form of Muhammad) has been one of the most common Turkish boy names for centuries. Today Mehmet, Mustafa, Ahmet, Yusuf, and Emre rotate near the top of Turkish naming statistics.

What is the most common Turkish girl name?

Ayşe (Aisha), Fatma (Fatima), Zeynep (Zaynab), and Elif lead the modern Turkish girl-name rankings. Elif is the Turkish form of the Arabic letter Alif and is purely Turkish in its modern usage.
