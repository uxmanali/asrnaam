Dictionary Meaning

What Dua means

Supplication, the believer's prayer, the direct call to Allah — from the root د-ع-و (da'a — to call, to invoke). Du'a is one of the central acts of Islamic worship, distinguished from salah (the formal ritual prayer) by being the unscripted, intimate calling out of the believer to Allah. The Prophet ﷺ said: 'Du'a is worship itself' (Tirmidhi, Abu Dawud) — and: 'Nothing repels the divine decree except du'a' (Tirmidhi).

The Quran is filled with the du'as of the prophets, each one a model: Adam's 'Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves' (7:23), Nuh's 'My Lord, forgive me' (71:28), Ibrahim's 'Our Lord, accept this from us' (2:127), Yunus's 'There is no god but You — glory be to You — indeed I have been of the wrongdoers' (21:87), Musa's 'My Lord, expand my chest for me' (20:25), and the believers': 'Our Lord, do not place upon us a burden like that which You laid upon those before us' (2:286).

The name Dua as a girls name expresses the bearer's identity as someone whose life is a continuous calling to Allah. Top Pakistani, Egyptian, Levantine, and Maghrebi girls name; particularly fashionable across the 2010s and 2020s. Variants: Duaa, Du'a, Doa, Doua. The Arabic is دُعَاء.

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run open-doored and receptive in how they greet people. The Ain that runs through the name points to a perceiving, seeing quality that looks past the surface — someone who tends to see what others miss in the room. The closing Hamza suggests a person who lands clearly and definitively.

The Portrait

What Dua asks of its bearer

The first letter of Dua is Dal — an outward self that opens doorways. Through the interior, Ain brings an interior of seeing; Alif brings an originary axis at the interior. These are the forces that shape the inner life. Dua ends in Alif — everything resolves back to the source. Read together, the letters ask her to embody prayer, supplication as a lived structure, not merely a definition.
