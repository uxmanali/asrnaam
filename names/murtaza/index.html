Dictionary Meaning

What Murtaza means

The one Allah is pleased with — from root ر-ض-و (rida — divine pleasure). مرتضى (Murtaza — the one with whom Allah is pleased, the approved). This is the title of Ali ibn Abi Talib (RA) — the Prophet's cousin, son-in-law, and fourth Caliph.

Murtaza is one of the top 30 searched Muslim boy names in Pakistan, South Asia, and Iran. It carries the aspiration to the highest station: being the one of whom Allah says 'I am pleased.'

The Letters

Reading Murtaza through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

مMeemFirst · Outward SelfرRaInteriorتTaInteriorضDadInteriorىYaLast · Resolution

Murtaza opens with Meem (م) — love. Then Ra (ر) — perpetual mercy. Then Ta (ت) — manifestation. Then Dad (ض) — authority. Then Ya at rest (ى) — arrived. Love — mercy — manifestation — authority — complete. The pleasing one: grounded in love, moving with mercy, manifesting visibly, authoritative, arrived complete.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi

The Portrait

What Murtaza asks of its bearer

People named Murtaza tend to carry a quality of genuine divine alignment — others sense they carry something approved. The Meem holds in love. The Ra moves with mercy. The Ta manifests. The Dad gives authority. The Ya at rest arrives complete.

A Notable Bearer

Ali ibn Abi Talib (RA) — Murtaza, fourth Caliph. 'I am the city of knowledge and Ali is its gate' — the Prophet ﷺ. 'Ali is to me as Aaron was to Moses.'

What does the name Murtaza mean?

Is Murtaza mentioned in the Quran?
Root ر-ض-و appears throughout the Quran. 'Allah is pleased with them and they are pleased with Him' (9:100). 'The pleasure of Allah is the greatest' (9:72). The highest station is to have Allah's pleasure.

Yes, Murtaza is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: The one Allah is pleased with — the beloved title of Ali ibn Abi Talib. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
