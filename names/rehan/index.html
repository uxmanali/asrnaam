Dictionary Meaning

What Rehan means

Fragrant herb, sweet basil — from root ر-ي-ح (raha — the soul, the spirit; also fragrance). ريحان (Raihan/Rehan — sweet basil, the fragrant herb). The Quran uses this word in Surah Al-Rahman: 'And the grain with husks and fragrant plants (raihan)' (55:12). And in Surah Al-Waqiah: 'Then rest and bounty and a garden of delight (raihan)' (56:89) — describing what awaits the near ones of Allah.

Rehan is the South Asian pronunciation and spelling of Raihan — one of the most popular boys' names across Pakistan, India and Bangladesh. The fragrant herb whose scent fills the air without effort — the name of the person whose spiritual fragrance reaches those around him.

The Letters

Reading Rehan through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

رRaFirst · Outward SelfيYaInteriorحHaInteriorاAlifInteriorنNunLast · Resolution

Rehan opens with Ra (ر) — perpetual mercy. Then Ya (ي) — the reaching hand. Then Ha (ح) — the breath of life, divine intimacy. Then Alif (ا) — origin. Then Nun (ن) — primordial depth. Perpetual mercy — reaching — divine breath of life — origin — depth. The fragrant herb: moving perpetually with mercy, reaching outward, breathing divine intimacy, rooted in origin, held in depth. Fragrance breathes and reaches — and it comes from the deepest root.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Rehan asks of its bearer

People named Rehan tend to carry a quality of spiritual fragrance — something about their presence sweetens the atmosphere without effort. The Ra moves with perpetual mercy. The Ya reaches. The Ha breathes divine intimacy. The Nun holds in depth: their fragrance comes from a deep source that does not run out.

A Notable Bearer

The raihan (fragrant herb) is mentioned in Surah Al-Rahman alongside the other gifts of divine creation. 'And for him who is of the near ones — rest and sweet basil and a garden of bliss' — Quran 56:89. Rehan names the man who carries this quality of spiritual fragrance.

Frequently Asked Questions

About the name Rehan

What does the name Rehan mean?
Rehan means: Fragrant herb, sweet basil — the name of the fragrant plant of paradise. From root ر-ي-ح (fragrance, soul). The sweet basil — the fragrant herb whose scent spreads without effort. A Quranic name mentioned in Surah Al-Rahman.
Is Rehan mentioned in the Quran?
Yes. Raihan is mentioned in the Quran in Surah Al-Rahman (55:12): 'And the grain with husks and fragrant plants (raihan)' — and in Surah Al-Waqiah (56:89): 'Then for him is rest and bounty and a garden of bliss.' The fragrant herb is one of the blessings of creation mentioned in the Quran.
What does Rehan mean in Urdu?
Rehan in Urdu is ریحان (Rehan) meaning خوشبودار پودا، ریحان — fragrant herb, sweet basil.
Is Rehan a good Islamic name?
Yes, Rehan is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: Fragrant herb, sweet basil — the name of the fragrant plant of paradise. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
Is Rehan a boy or girl name?
Rehan is traditionally a Muslim boy name meaning Fragrant herb, sweet basil — the name of the fragrant plant of paradise.
What is the spiritual meaning of Rehan in Ilm ul Huroof?
Ra (ر) gives perpetual mercy, Ya (ي) reaches outward, Ha (ح) breathes divine intimacy, Alif (ا) grounds in origin, Nun (ن) holds in depth. Fragrance: merciful, reaching, breathing the divine, rooted, deep.
