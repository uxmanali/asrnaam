Muslim Baby Names Mentioned in the Quran

Names of prophets, companions, Quranic women, and Quranic words — each with its Arabic spelling, classical meaning, and a letter-by-letter reading through Ilm ul Huroof.

78 names

The Quran preserves the names of twenty-five prophets and a small number of women (Maryam by name, and others through their stories — Asiya, Hawa, Sara, Hajra). Beyond the prophets, the Quran's vocabulary itself — words like al-Nūr (the Light), al-Hudā (guidance), al-Sabr (patience), al-Salām (peace) — has long supplied Muslim families with names that carry the resonance of revelation.

Choosing a name with a Quranic anchor is a way of placing the child inside a recognised tradition. The Prophet ﷺ encouraged beautiful names, and the names borne by the Anbiyāʾ are, in every classical source, among the most beloved. The names below are grouped first by the prophets the Quran mentions explicitly, then by women whose names are preserved either directly or through their stories, then companions whose names became Quranic anchors through their proximity to the revelation, then Quranic words used as names in their own right.

Each entry links to its full reading on AsrNaam: the Arabic spelling, the dictionary meaning, the Quranic context (with verse references where applicable), the Urdu form, and the Ilm ul Huroof letter-by-letter reading.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Quranic names?

Quranic names are Muslim names that are either mentioned directly in the Quran — like the prophets (Muhammad, Ibrahim, Yusuf, Musa, Isa, Maryam) and the Quranic figures (Asiya, Luqman, Imran) — or are drawn from Quranic vocabulary, such as Nur (light), Huda (guidance), Salam (peace), and Rahma (mercy).

How many prophets are named in the Quran?

The Quran mentions twenty-five prophets by name: Adam, Idris, Nuh, Hud, Salih, Ibrahim, Ismail, Ishaq, Yaqub, Yusuf, Ayyub, Shu'ayb, Musa, Harun, Dawud, Sulaiman, Ilyas, Alyasa, Dhulkifl, Yunus, Zakariyya, Yahya, Isa, and Muhammad. Many Muslim families name children after them.

Is it good to name a child after a Quranic figure?

Yes. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ encouraged giving children beautiful and meaningful names, and the names of the prophets and Quranic figures carry the strongest classical anchors in Islamic tradition. Each name page on AsrNaam shows the verse references and Ilm ul Huroof letter reading.

What does the Quran say about names?

The Quran emphasises the significance of naming — the name of Allah opens every chapter, prophets are introduced by name, and the divine names (al-Asmā' al-Husnā) form the basis for many Muslim names. The Prophet ﷺ instructed: 'On the Day of Judgment you will be called by your names and your fathers' names, so make your names beautiful.'
