Dictionary Meaning

What Wafa means

Faithfulness, loyalty, the fulfilment of what was promised — from root و-ف-ي (wafa — to be faithful, to fulfil a promise, to be loyal). وفاء (Wafa — faithfulness, the complete fulfilment of promise and loyalty). The Quran commands: 'Wa awfu bil-ahdi, inna al-ahda kana mas'ula — fulfil the covenant, for the covenant will be questioned' (17:34).

Wafa names the woman of total faithfulness — whose loyalty does not depend on conditions, whose promise is kept even when difficult, whose commitment is the bedrock others build upon.

PronunciationWA-fa  ·  wa·fa  ·  2 syllables

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Wafa reveals

Waw of connection, Fa of opening, Alif of return. Fidelity opening at the source.

People often notice a connective quality, tending to bridge between people in those who carry this name.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run a connector who pulls people together easily. The Fa that runs through the name points to a discerning, sorting interior that separates the real from the noise — someone who is trusted to give honest counsel. The closing Hamza suggests a person who lands clearly and definitively.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

The Portrait

What Wafa asks of its bearer

People named Wafa tend to be the most reliable presences — their word is kept, their loyalty is total. The Waw connects: their faithfulness is itself a connection. The Fa opens spiritually. The Alif grounds in origin. The Hamza close begins strongly: their faithfulness is not tentative.
