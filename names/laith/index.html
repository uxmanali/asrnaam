Dictionary Meaning

What Laith means

Lion — from root ل-ي-ث (layitha — to be lion-like, to be powerful). ليث (laith — lion, the powerful one). In Arabic, laith is one of the many words for lion, carrying particular connotations of the lion's protective quality — the lion that defends its territory and those within.

Laith ibn Sa'd (713–791 CE) was one of the greatest Islamic scholars of his era — the Imam of Egypt, considered by Imam al-Shafi'i to be more learned than Imam Malik. The name carries both the warrior quality and the scholarly lion.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Laith

ل-ي-ث — From l-y-th — the lion; classical Arabic has many lion-words, each with a flavor.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Laith reveals

Lam authority, Ya reaching, Tha spreading. Authority that reaches and spreads — strength as influence, not domination.

How This Shows Up in Life

Personality & relational pattern

People named Laith tend to be drawing others in through warmth and attachment. The Ya that runs through the name points to a reaching, aspirational interior — someone who draws people upward toward what they're moving toward. The closing Tha suggests a person who endures into the long term and outlasts the quick wins.

An interpretive reading drawn from the letters of the name — for reflection, not prediction.

Reading Laith through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

The Portrait

What Laith asks of its bearer

People named Laith tend to have a quiet, protective courage — they are the ones who stand between the vulnerable and what threatens them. The Lam gives divine authority: their courage is not self-appointed. The Ya reaches. The Tha close multiplies: their protective quality spreads.

A Notable Bearer

Laith ibn Sa'd (713–791 CE) — the great Imam of Egypt, one of the most learned scholars of the classical era. Imam al-Shafi'i said of him: 'Laith ibn Sa'd is more learned than Malik, but his students did not preserve his knowledge as Malik's students preserved his.'

Famous Bearers

Notable people who have carried the name Laith.
