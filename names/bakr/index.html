Dictionary Meaning

What Bakr means

Bakr (بكر, Bakr) is an Arabic masculine name meaning young she-camel, virgin (untouched), the firstborn — and is best known as the personal name of Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq (born c. 573 CE, d. 634 CE), the first Caliph of Islam, the Prophet Muhammad's closest companion, the second of the two when they took refuge in the cave of Thawr (Qur'an 9:40). Abū Bakr was the first adult male to embrace Islam, the father of ʿĀʾisha bint Abī Bakr, and called al-Ṣiddīq (the Truthful) — for he affirmed the Night Journey without hesitation. The Prophet ﷺ said: "If I were to take a close friend (khalīl) other than my Lord, I would have taken Abū Bakr" (Bukhārī). The kunya Abū Bakr ("father of Bakr") was given because Bakr was an honored Arabian name long before Islam. As a personal name today, Bakr (or Bakar, Bekir Turkish, Boker) remains in use across the Arab world, Turkey, and Indonesia, especially in compound forms (Abū Bakr, Bakr Allāh).
