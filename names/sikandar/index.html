Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

Persianised form of Greek Alexandros (Alexander). Sikandar Dhū al-Qarnayn appears in classical Islamic literature.

A Greek royal name adopted into the Persianate-Islamic world; sometimes identified with the Quranic Dhū al-Qarnayn.

Read through the Science of Letters, Sikandar opens with Seen (س), the letter of movement, and resolves on Dal (د), the letter of door. Between the two stand origin, covering, origin, depth, door., a chord of interior qualities the name carries forward.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Sikandar brings air and water together, the elements of clear seeing and feeling deeply. The bearer is asked to name what is felt without cooling it, and to feel what is thought without losing it. Anchoring the reading is Al-Sami, the All-Hearing, meaning the person carries the alertness to hear what is not being said aloud. A person named Sikandar is often the one people can be honest with.

Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Sikandar Lodi (d. 1517 CE · Delhi). Second Lodi sultan of Delhi; founder of Agra (1504).
  • Sikandar Shah (Bengal) (d. 1390 CE · Pandua). Second sultan of the Ilyas Shahi dynasty of Bengal; commissioned the Adina Mosque at Pandua.
