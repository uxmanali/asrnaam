Dictionary Meaning

What Ghazala means

The gazelle — the swift desert antelope that classical Arab poetry treats as the supreme image of feminine grace and beauty. From the root غ-ز-ل (ghazala — the female gazelle). The same root gives al-ghazal (the love-poem), so named because love-poetry was the genre in which Arab poets compared their beloveds to gazelles — for the long neck, the dark eyes, the swift turning of the head, the alertness combined with grace.

The name does not appear in the Quran, but the gazelle is named in Hadith: the Prophet ﷺ used to drink from the same place a gazelle had drunk in Madinah, and a Hadith narrated by Abu Hurayra describes a gazelle speaking to the Prophet ﷺ to ask his help against a hunter who was about to kill her — a story classical scholars cite for the gazelle's place in Islamic moral imagination as a symbol of the gentle creature deserving protection.

The name Ghazala as a girls name carries the entire register of Arabic literary affection — beauty paired with alertness, gentleness paired with quick decisiveness. Top Pakistani, Egyptian, Levantine, and Maghrebi girls name. Famous bearer: Ghazala Javed, the Pakhtun Pakistani singer. Variants: Ghazaala, Ghazalah, Gazala, Gazel (Turkish). The Arabic is غَزَالَة.

The Portrait

What Ghazala asks of its bearer

At the opening of Ghazala stands Ghayn: an outward self of mystery. Through the interior, Zayn brings an interior beauty; Alif brings an originary axis at the interior. These are the forces that shape the inner life. The Alif resolves the name: everything resolves back to the source. The work of the name is to let gazelle live through every letter — so that her life takes the same shape as her name.
