Pakistani Muslim Girl Names, Urdu, Arabic & Persian Heritage

Forty Pakistani Muslim girl names, the most-loved Urdu, Arabic, and Persian forms used across Pakistan, with their Arabic spelling, meaning, and full Ilm ul Huroof reading.

46 names

Pakistani naming for girls draws on three streams. Arabic-Quranic forms (Maryam, Khadija, Aisha, Fatima, Zainab) carry the direct Islamic anchor. Persian-Mughal forms (Gulnaz, Mehwish, Mehnaz, Naseem) carry the poetic register inherited from the Mughal court and the centuries of Persian as the courtly language of the subcontinent. Urdu-native forms (Hareem, Mehreen, Areej, Anaya, Inaya) carry the more recent contributions of Urdu literary culture and modern Pakistani naming taste.

The same name often crosses streams. Aisha is written عائشہ in Urdu rather than the Arabic عائشة, but the meaning, the Quranic anchor, and the Ilm ul Huroof reading are the same. Each entry below links to its full canonical page with the Urdu form rendered alongside the Arabic.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most popular Pakistani Muslim girl name?

Aisha (عائشہ), Fatima, Maryam, Khadija, and Zainab, the names of the Prophet ﷺ's wives, daughter, and granddaughter, lead Pakistani girl-name rankings every year, alongside modern Urdu favourites like Hareem, Mehreen, Anaya, and Inaya.

What are unique Pakistani girl names?

Pakistani girl naming draws on three streams: Quranic Arabic (Maryam, Khadija), Persian-Mughal (Gulnaz, Mehwish, Mehnaz, Naseem), and modern Urdu (Hareem, Areej, Anaya, Inaya, Nimra). Each carries an Arabic spelling, classical meaning, and Ilm ul Huroof letter reading.

What are Pakistani girl names with deep meaning?

Names like Maryam (the Virgin Mary), Hidayah (divine guidance), Sakinah (tranquillity from Allah), Rahma (mercy), Noor (divine light), and Kawthar (the Quranic river of paradise) carry strong Islamic and classical Arabic meanings.

Is Aisha a Pakistani name?

Aisha is fundamentally an Arabic Islamic name, borne by ʿĀʾisha bint Abī Bakr, wife of the Prophet ﷺ. It is one of the most popular girl names in Pakistan, written عائشہ in Urdu. The Urdu form and the Arabic عائشة share the same meaning, the same Quranic-era anchor, and the same Ilm ul Huroof letter reading.
