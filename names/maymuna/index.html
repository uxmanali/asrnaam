Dictionary Meaning

What Maymuna means

Blessed, auspicious, lucky — from root ي-م-ن (yamana — to be blessed, to be auspicious). ميمونة (maymuna — the blessed one, the auspicious). Maymuna bint al-Harith (RA) was the last woman the Prophet ﷺ married — one of the Mothers of the Believers. Ibn Abbas (RA) and Khalid ibn al-Walid (RA) were her nephews.

Maymuna was known for her exceptional generosity and piety. The Prophet ﷺ died in her apartment on his turn, though he was ill. The name carries the quality of the woman whose life brings blessing.

The Letters

Reading Maymuna through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

مMeemFirst · Outward SelfيYaInteriorمMeemInteriorوWawInteriorنNunInteriorهHaLast · Resolution

Maymuna opens with Meem (م) — love. Then Ya (ي) — reaching. Then Meem (م) — love again. Then Waw (و) — connection. Then Nun (ن) — depth. Then Ha (ه) — divine presence. Love — reaching — love — connection — depth — divine presence. The blessed: framed in double love, reaching, connecting, held in depth, breathing divine presence.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

What Maymuna asks of its bearer

People named Maymuna tend to carry a quality of blessing — things tend to go better when they are present. The double Meem gives double love. The Waw connects. The Nun holds in depth. The Ha breathes divine presence: her blessing is real.

A Notable Bearer

Maymuna bint al-Harith (RA) — the last wife of the Prophet ﷺ, Mother of the Believers. Her nephews included Ibn Abbas (RA), the greatest Quran commentator, and Khalid ibn al-Walid (RA), the greatest military commander.
