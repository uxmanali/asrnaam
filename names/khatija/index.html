Dictionary Meaning

What Khatija means

Premature child, born early — from root خ-د-ج (khadaja — to be born prematurely). Khadija bint Khuwaylid (RA) was the first wife of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, the first person to believe in his prophethood, and the greatest woman of her era. A successful businesswoman of Makkah, she was twice widowed when she proposed marriage to the Prophet.

For 25 years she was his only wife. She supported him through every difficulty, spent her wealth for Islam, and died before the Hijra. The Prophet ﷺ said: 'She believed in me when everyone else disbelieved; she accepted me when everyone else rejected me; she helped me with her wealth when everyone else deprived me.'

How This Shows Up in Life

Personality & relational pattern

People named Khatija tend to be attentive, with a sense that they see more than they say. The Dal that runs through the name points to a returning quality — they come back to what matters — someone who leaves doors open in relationships, slow to write people off. The closing Ta suggests a person who completes things cleanly, with few loose ends.

The Letters

Reading Khatija through Ilm ul Huroof

Read letter by letter, Khatija unfolds in stages: the first marks how the bearer appears; the middle ones hold what is private; the last says how the journey closes.

Khatija opens with Kha (خ) — the hidden interior. Then Dal (د) — the door, return. Then Ya (ي) — reaching. Then Jeem (ج) — comprehensive beauty. Then Ha (ه) — divine presence. Hidden interior — returning door — reaching — comprehensive beauty — divine presence. The first believer: drawing from hidden depth, always returning to what is essential, reaching toward the Prophet, gathering all beauty, breathing divine presence.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the name Khatija mean?

Khatija means: Premature child — first wife of the Prophet, greatest woman of her era. In Islamic tradition, this name carries deep significance rooted in Arabic etymology and spiritual heritage.

Is Khatija a good Islamic name?

Yes, Khatija is an excellent Islamic name. The Prophet ﷺ encouraged giving children beautiful names with good meanings. Khatija carries premature child — first wife of the prophet, greatest woman of her era, making it a blessed and meaningful choice.

Is Khatija mentioned in the Quran?

No. Khadija is not mentioned by name in the Quran. She is the first wife of the Prophet ﷺ, the first Muslim, and one of the four greatest women in human history according to Islamic tradition.

What is the spiritual meaning of the name Khatija?
