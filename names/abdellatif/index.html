Dictionary Meaning

What Abdellatif means

Abdellatif (عبد اللطيف) is composed of two Arabic words: ʿabd — servant, wholly at the disposal of another — and the divine Name اللطيف, the Subtle, the One whose kindness reaches the smallest places. The full name therefore declares its bearer the servant of the Subtle, the One whose kindness reaches the smallest places.

"He is the Subtle, the All-Aware — al-Laṭīf, al-Khabīr" (67:14). The Prophet ﷺ said, in a hadith reported by Abu Dawud and Ibn Mājah: "The most beloved of names to Allah are ʿAbdullāh and ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān" — and the classical scholars extended that love to every name of the form ʿAbd-X where X is one of the asmāʾ al-ḥusnā. The romanizations vary: Abdellatif is the Maghrebi (North African) form, while Abdul Latif predominates in the Arab East and South Asia. The same root letters and the same theological meaning sit beneath every spelling.

The Portrait

What Abdellatif asks of its bearer

To carry Abdellatif is to receive a vocation: to incarnate, in a small way, tenderness that arrives by hidden paths. The early servants of this Name were not passive — they acted in the world — but they read every act as the act of a servant. The bearer is asked to live so that the Name pointed to in his name is recognisable in him.
