Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Al-Dinawari.

  • Abū Ḥanīfa Aḥmad ibn Dāwūd al-Dīnawarī (c. 815–895 CE · Dīnawar (Jibāl, western Iran) / Isfahan / Baghdad). Abū Ḥanīfa al-Dīnawarī produced the founding work of Arabic botany. Kitāb al-Nabāt (Book of Plants), in six volumes, of which substantial portions survive. The work catalogues hundreds of species with their morphology, habitat, agricultural uses, and Arabic and Persian names, drawing on field observation, Bedouin lore, and the existing Greek and Persian botanical literature. He also composed Kitāb al-Akhbār al-Ṭiwāl, a universal history particularly valuable for pre-Islamic Persia.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Al-Dinawari reveals

Al-Dinawari opens with guidance (Dāl) and depth (Nūn) and resolves in receptive form (Yā). A name fitting for a scholar whose Book of Plants catalogued the living forms of the land with patient attention and returned them to careful description.

Legacy

What Al-Dinawari left behind

Kitāb al-Nabāt is the first systematic botanical work in Arabic and a primary source for later writers including al-Bīrūnī and Ibn al-Bayṭār. Al-Dīnawarī's combination of philological precision with field observation set the methodological pattern for Arabic natural science for centuries afterward.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
