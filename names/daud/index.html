Dictionary Meaning

What Daud means

Daud (داود, Dāwūd) is the Arabic name of the Prophet David — one of the great prophet-kings of the Banī Isrāʾīl, mentioned by name 16 times in the Qur'an. He defeated Goliath (Jālūt) as a young man (Qur'an 2:251), was given the kingdom and wisdom, and was the recipient of al-Zabūr (the Psalms, Qur'an 4:163, 17:55). The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ praised the worship of Dāwūd: he fasted alternate days and slept half the night, prayed a third, and slept a sixth — calling this "the most beloved fast and prayer to Allah" (Bukhārī, Muslim). The mountains and birds joined Dāwūd in glorifying Allah (Qur'an 21:79, 34:10). Daud is the standard South Asian and Indonesian-Malaysian transliteration (Indonesian Daud, Malay Daud); Dawood, Dawud, and Daoud are used in the Arab world. The English form David shares the same Hebrew origin (דָּוִד, "beloved"). Among the most widely used prophet-names in the Muslim world after Muhammad, Ibrahim, Ismail, and Yusuf.

The Letters

Reading Daud through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

دDalFirst · Outward SelfاAlifInteriorوWawInteriorدDalLast · Resolution

Dal (returning door) — Alif (origin) — Waw (connection) — Dal (returning again). Prophet-King: returning, originary, connecting, returning. Double Dal: doubly returning.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."
