Kabir — Great, immense — one of the divine names of overwhelming greatness

What the name Kabir means, what its letters say, and what it asks of the person who carries it.

Dictionary Meaning

What Kabir means

Great, large, immense — from root ك-ب-ر (kabura — to be great, to be of high status). كبير (kabir — great, old, of high status). Al-Kabir is one of the divine names. The Quran: 'Wa Allahu al-Kabir al-Mutakabbir' — and Allah is the Great, the Supremely Great.

Kabir names the man of genuine greatness — not social status but real weight of character. The 15th-century poet-saint Kabir of India is one of the most celebrated Islamic figures in South Asian history, whose devotional poetry bridged communities.

The Letters

Reading Kabir through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

كKafFirst · Outward SelfبBaInteriorيYaInteriorرRaLast · Resolution

Kabir opens with Kaf (ك) — sufficiency. Then Ba (ب) — the vessel. Then Ya (ي) — reaching. Then Ra (ر) — perpetual mercy. Sufficiency — vessel — reaching — perpetual mercy. The great: drawing from divine sufficiency, becoming a vessel for greatness, reaching outward, moving perpetually with mercy. True greatness is a vessel for something greater.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi

The Portrait

What Kabir asks of its bearer

People named Kabir tend to have a quality of genuine weight — their presence and words carry more than surface meaning. The Kaf gives them sufficiency. The Ba makes them a vessel. The Ra close moves perpetually with mercy: their greatness is merciful.

A Notable Bearer

Al-Kabir — the Great — one of the divine names. Kabir (1440–1518) — the Indian Muslim poet-saint whose devotional poetry in Hindi became beloved across all communities.
