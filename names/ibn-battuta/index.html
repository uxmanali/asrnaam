Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

From the family name بَطّوطَة (Baṭṭūṭa), of disputed origin, possibly a diminutive of بَطّ (baṭṭ, duck) or a Berber familial term arabised.

Ibn Battuta carries Ba (ب) of containment and Ṭa (ط) of cyclical return. A name shaped to leave home and to come back changed by what was seen.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Ibn Battuta is grounded in earth, the element of stability, patience, and quiet holding. The letters ask their bearer to be reliable rather than showy, to keep the household of the heart in order. Stitched through this reading is Al-Ahad, the One, meaning the person carries a singular, undivided presence. A person named Ibn Battuta is the household others rest against without noticing they are resting.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

Son of Baṭṭūṭa, a duck, or a familial nickname of obscure origin. From the family name بَطّوطَة (Baṭṭūṭa), of disputed origin, possibly a diminutive of بَطّ (baṭṭ, duck) or a Berber familial term arabised.

Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Abū ʿAbdullāh Muḥammad ibn ʿAbdullāh ibn Baṭṭūṭa al-Lawātī al-Ṭanjī (1304–1369 CE · Tangier (Maghreb) / Delhi / Mali). Ibn Battuta set out from Tangier in 1325 at the age of twenty-one for the hajj and did not return for twenty-nine years. His Riḥla (Journey) records travels across the Maghreb, Egypt, Arabia, the Levant, Anatolia, the Eurasian steppe, Central Asia, the Indian subcontinent, where he served as a qadi under the Delhi Sultanate, the Maldives, Sri Lanka, Southeast Asia, China, sub-Saharan Mali, and Iberia. Total distance: approximately 120,000 kilometres, three times Marco Polo's range.
