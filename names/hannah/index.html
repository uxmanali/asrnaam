Dictionary Meaning

What Hannah means

Happiness, contentment, grace — from root ه-ن-أ (hana'a — to be in a state of happiness and wholesome ease). هناء (Hannah — happiness, contentment). The double-n spelling Hannah is both the Islamic Hana/Hanaa name and the Biblical Hebrew name of the Prophet Samuel's mother (Hannah/Hanna) — making it one of the most cross-traditional names in existence.

Hannah is the #21 most searched Muslim baby name on QuranicNames.com in 2026 (26%), capturing the enormous audience of Muslim parents who want both the Islamic meaning (happiness, ease, the Quranic hani'an — 'eat and drink with contentment' 52:19) and the Biblical heritage (the mother of Samuel who prayed for a son). A universally beloved name across all traditions.

The Letters

Reading Hannah through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

هHaFirst · Outward SelfنNunInteriorاAlifInteriorءHamzaLast · Resolution

Hannah: Ha (ه) — divine presence. Nun (ن) — primordial depth. Alif (ا) — origin. Hamza (ء) — strong beginning. Divine presence — primordial depth — origin — strong beginning. Happiness: breathing divine presence, held in primordial depth, rooted in origin, and beginning strongly. True happiness breathes the divine, comes from depth, is originary, and begins with strength.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Hannah asks of its bearer

People named Hannah carry a quality of deep, genuine contentment — their happiness is rooted and authentic. The Ha breathes divine presence: her happiness is divinely sourced. The Nun holds in primordial depth. The Alif grounds in origin. The Hamza begins strongly.

A Notable Bearer

Hannah bint Faqudha — grandmother of Maryam (AS) and great-grandmother of Eisa (AS) in Islamic tradition. She prayed for a righteous child and was granted Maryam. The same Hannah who is the mother of Samuel in the Biblical tradition.

Frequently Asked Questions

About the name Hannah

What does the name Hannah mean?
Hannah means: Happiness, contentment — the Biblical-Islamic name of profound spiritual joy. From root ه-ن-أ (happy, wholesome contentment). 'Eat and drink with contentment (hani'an)' — Quran 52:19. Also the Biblical mother of Samuel, grandmother of Maryam (AS) in Islamic tradition. #21 searched 2026 (26%).
Is Hannah mentioned in the Quran?
Root ه-ن-أ appears in the Quran: 'Eat and drink with wholesome contentment (hani'an) for what you did in days past' (69:24, 52:19) — describing the ease of paradise. Hannah also connects to the Biblical mother of Samuel, referenced in Islamic tradition as the grandmother of Maryam (AS).
What does Hannah mean in Urdu?
Hannah in Urdu is ہنا (Hannah) meaning خوشی، سکون — happiness, contentment, grace.
Is Hannah a good Islamic name?
Yes, Hannah is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: Happiness, contentment — the Biblical-Islamic name of profound spiritual joy. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
Is Hannah a girl or boy name?
Hannah is traditionally a Muslim girl name meaning Happiness, contentment — the Biblical-Islamic name of profound spiritual joy.
What is the spiritual meaning of Hannah in Ilm ul Huroof?
Ha (ه) breathes divine presence, Nun (ن) holds in depth, Alif (ا) grounds, Hamza (ء) begins strongly. Happiness: divinely present, deep, originary, strongly beginning.
