Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Ibn Khaldun weaves earth and water, the elements of steadiness and mercy, patience and feeling. The person is asked to be both root and river, a slow, sheltering presence. Running through the reading is Al-Ahad, the One, meaning the bearer is trusted with a singular, undivided presence, joined by Al-Nur, the Light, carried as a light that steadies the room. A person named Ibn Khaldun is the family's slow heart.

Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Abū Zayd ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Muḥammad ibn Khaldūn al-Ḥaḍramī (1332–1406 CE · Tunis / Granada / Cairo). The Muqaddima (Prolegomena) to Ibn Khaldun's universal history, Kitāb al-ʿIbar, is the founding text of historical sociology. He proposed ʿaṣabiyya, group solidarity, as the engine of dynastic rise and decline; analysed the role of climate and economy in shaping civilisation; described the labour theory of value four centuries before Adam Smith; and identified the fiscal stages of empire (taxation, decline, fall) with a precision that twentieth-century economic historians have rediscovered.

Legacy

What Ibn Khaldun left behind

Arnold Toynbee called the Muqaddima 'the greatest work of its kind ever created by any mind in any time or place.' Modern social scientists from Ernest Gellner to Charles Issawi have built directly on Ibn Khaldun's framework. The University of Tunis carries his name; the Egyptian, Tunisian and Algerian currencies have all carried his face.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
