Dictionary Meaning

What Fouad means

The heart — the Quranic fu'ad, from the root ف-أ-د (fa'ada). The Quran uses this exact word for the heart as the seat of conscious feeling and faith. Fu'ad is distinguished in classical Arabic from qalb (the more general word for heart): fu'ad is the heart in its quality of warmth, attachment, and active devotion.

The Quran uses this word at decisive moments: 'And the heart (fu'ad) did not lie about what it saw' (53:11) — describing the Prophet's sight of the angel Jibril at the Lote-Tree of the Boundary; 'And the heart of Musa's mother became empty (faraghan)' (28:10) — describing the unbearable longing of a mother who had just placed her infant on the river. The Quran also names al-af'ida (the plural) as one of the three faculties Allah gave human beings: 'And Allah brought you out of the wombs of your mothers knowing nothing, and gave you hearing, sight, and hearts (al-af'ida)' (16:78) — making the heart, alongside hearing and sight, one of the three foundations of accountability.

The name Fouad as a personal name carries the entire register of the Quranic heart. Top Egyptian, Levantine, and Maghrebi boys name; especially associated with Egypt where King Fuad I (r. 1922-1936) and King Fuad II (r. 1952-1953) bore the name. Variants: Fuad, Fouad, Foaad, Fuat (Turkish). The Arabic is فُؤَاد.
