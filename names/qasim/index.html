What the name Qasim means, what its letters say, and what it asks of the person who carries it.

Dictionary Meaning

What Qasim means

Distributor, the one who divides and shares — from root ق-س-م (qasama — to divide, to distribute, to share). قاسم (qasim — the one who distributes, who divides justly and shares). The divine name Al-Qasim is used in the sense of the divine distributor of provisions.

Qasim was the name of the Prophet's ﷺ first son — who died in infancy — from whom the Prophet took his kunya (paternal title) Abu Qasim (the father of Qasim). Qasim ibn Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr (657-726 CE) was the grandson of Abu Bakr (RA) and one of the seven great jurists of Madinah. The name carries the quality of the just distributor — the one who gives everyone their due portion.

The Letters

Reading Qasim through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

قQafFirst · Outward SelfاAlifInteriorسSinInteriorمMeemLast · Resolution

Qasim opens with Qaf (ق) — the divine axis, the cosmic pole. Then Alif (ا) — origin. Then Sin (س) — flowing expansion. Then Meem (م) — love and depth. The divine pole — origin — flowing expansion — love and depth. The distributor: grounded in the absolute divine axis, rooted in origin, flowing in expansion, resolved in love and depth. The just distributor stands at the absolute centre and gives outward in all directions.

The Portrait

What Qasim asks of its bearer

People named Qasim tend to have a quality of just distribution — they are the ones who divide fairly, who ensure everyone receives their portion. The Qaf grounds them in the absolute: their distribution is not arbitrary but oriented toward what is true. The Sin gives them flowing expansion. The Meem close resolves in love: their giving comes from genuine love.

A Notable Bearer

Qasim — the Prophet's ﷺ first son, who died in infancy, giving the Prophet his kunya Abu Qasim. Qasim ibn Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr — one of the seven great jurists of Madinah, grandson of Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (RA).
