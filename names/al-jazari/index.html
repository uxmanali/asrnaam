Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Al-Jazari's letters draw from many elements at once, a full palette, not a single note. This is a name that asks its bearer to hold contradictions gracefully: warmth and steadiness, movement and depth. Running through the reading is Al-Ahad, the One, carried by a person as a singular, undivided presence. A person named Al-Jazari lives with more inside them than any one word can hold.

Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Badīʿ al-Zamān Abū al-ʿIzz Ismāʿīl ibn al-Razzāz al-Jazarī (c. 1136–1206 CE · Diyar Bakr (Upper Mesopotamia) / Hisn Kayfa (Artuqid court)). Al-Jazarī served at the Artuqid court at Hisn Kayfa for some twenty-five years and, at the request of the Artuqid prince, composed in 1206 CE his Kitāb fī Maʿrifat al-Ḥiyal al-Handasiyya, the Book of Knowledge of Ingenious Mechanical Devices. The work describes some fifty machines in six categories: water-clocks, vessels for drinking, jugs and basins for ritual washing, fountains and musical automata, water-raising devices, and miscellaneous devices, each illustrated with the author's own diagrams.
