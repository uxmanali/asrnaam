Pakistani Muslim Boy Names, Quranic, Arabic & Pashto Heritage

Pakistani Muslim boy names, Quranic prophets and companions, Arabic classical names, Persian-Mughal forms, and Pashto/Punjabi favourites, with full Ilm ul Huroof letter readings.

49 names

Pakistan inherits its naming directly from the centre of the Muslim world. The Quranic prophets (Muhammad, Ibrahim, Ismail, Yusuf, Musa, Isa) and the Companions (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali) make up the densest cluster of Pakistani boy names. Layered on top is the Mughal-era Persian register (Daniyal, Faraz, Sohail, Zahid, Zia) and a strong Pashto/Punjabi contribution from the north and west (Sayhan, Zaviyar, Shahbaz, Akbar).

Names like Muhammad and Ahmad are often used as honorific prefixes, Muhammad Ali, Muhammad Hamza, where the Quranic name precedes the calling name. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most popular Pakistani Muslim boy name?

Muhammad, Ahmad, Ali, Hassan, Hussain, Omar, Usman, Hamza, and Ibrahim lead Pakistani boy-name rankings. Muhammad is the most-given boy name in the world, often used as an honorific prefix (e.g. Muhammad Ali, Muhammad Hamza).

What are modern Pakistani boy names?

Modern Pakistani boy naming blends Quranic anchors with Urdu, Persian, and Pashto contributions: Daniyal, Faraz, Sohail, Zaviyar, Sayhan, Ayan, Shahbaz, and Akbar are widely used today alongside the classical Quranic names.

What is a strong Islamic Pakistani name for a boy?

Names borne by the Prophet ﷺ and his Companions are the strongest Islamic anchors: Muhammad, Ahmad, Abdullah, Abdul Rahman, Ali, Umar, Uthman, Hamza, Khalid, Bilal, Salman. Each links to a Companion whose biography is preserved in the classical hadith literature.

Are Pakistani boy names Arabic or Urdu?

Most Pakistani boy names are Arabic in origin, the names of the Prophet ﷺ, his Companions, and the Quranic figures. They are written in Urdu script (e.g. محمد for Muhammad) and pronounced with South Asian phonology, but the underlying Arabic root, classical meaning, and Ilm ul Huroof letter reading is the same as the Arabic source.
