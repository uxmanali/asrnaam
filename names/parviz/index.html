Dictionary Meaning

What Parviz means

Victorious, lucky, blessed — from Persian پرویز (parviz — victorious, lucky, the fortunate one). The name of a quality of divine favour that brings consistent success and good fortune. Khosrow Parviz (Chosroes II) was the last great Sassanid Emperor of Persia (590-628 CE), to whom the Prophet ﷺ wrote a letter inviting him to Islam — a letter he reportedly tore up, leading to the fall of his empire.

Despite that historical association, Parviz/Parvez remains a beloved name in Persian and Urdu Muslim communities for its meaning of victorious good fortune.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Parviz

Persian origin — parvīz, victorious. Khusraw Parvīz was the last great Sasanian Persian emperor (ruled 590–628 CE), to whom the Prophet ﷺ sent a letter calling him to Islam.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Parviz reveals

Ba (Pa) of foundation opens, Ra of mercy, Waw of connection, Ya of reaching, Zayn of subtle radiance. Victory connected and reaching radiance.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run reserved but warm once approached. The Ra that runs through the name points to forward motion, a restlessness in good ways — someone who tends to lead or set the direction in a group. The closing Zayn suggests a person who concludes things on a note of brightness.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.
