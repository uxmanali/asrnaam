Umar Meaning in Urdu (عُمَر)

عُمَر. Long-lived, flourishing.

The name Umar (عُمَر) is written in Urdu as عُمَر. In the South Asian Muslim tradition, Umar carries the meaning of Long-lived, flourishing, drawn from the Arabic root ع-م-ر (ع-م-ر. From ʿ-m-r, to live long; the same root as ʿumr (life-span)). In the South Asian Muslim tradition, Umar is given with intention, the letters and the meaning together shape the bearer.

Is Umar Mentioned in the Quran?

Root ain-m-r (life, prosperity): 'And We gave them long life (wa ammarnakum)' (35:37). Umar's name connects to long, prosperous life. The Prophet's du'a for him was answered directly by his conversion.

Dictionary Meaning

What Umar means

The Distinguisher (Al-Faruq), the second Caliph of Islam. Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA) converted to Islam when the Muslims were few and weak, the Prophet prayed: 'O Allah, strengthen Islam with Abu Jahl or Umar ibn al-Khattab. And the more beloved of the two to Allah was Umar.' When he accepted Islam, the Muslims could pray openly at the Kaaba for the first time.

Under his 10-year caliphate (634-644 CE), the Islamic empire expanded from the Arabian Peninsula to Persia, Iraq, Syria, Palestine, and Egypt. He was famously ascetic despite ruling an empire, patching his own clothes, carrying flour to poor widows at night. The Prophet said: 'If there were a prophet after me, it would be Umar.' He established the Islamic calendar, the diwan (state treasury), and welfare payments for the poor.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run perceptive and watchful in how they meet people. The Meem that runs through the name points to a deep capacity for love and a strong tie to family and inner faith, someone who tends to become the emotional centre of the people around them. The closing Ra suggests a person who lets endings push into the next beginning.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.
