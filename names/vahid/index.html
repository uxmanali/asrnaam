Dictionary Meaning

What Vahid means

Unique, singular, the only one — from Arabic وحيد (wahid — unique, the one of a kind). Vahid is the Persian/Urdu/Turkish form. From root و-ح-د (wahada — to be one, to be unique). Al-Wahid is one of the divine names — the Unique One. Closely related to Ahad (the absolute One) — Wahid is one in number while Ahad is unique in essence.

Vahid names the man who is genuinely unique — the one of a kind who cannot be replaced. Used across Persian, Urdu, Turkish and broader Muslim communities.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Vahid

و-ح-د — Persian form of Waḥīd, from w-ḥ-d, to be one. Al-Wāḥid is one of the divine names: the One.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Vahid reveals

Waw of connection, Alif of origin, Ha-heavy of breath, Ya of reaching, Dal of return. Oneness reaching the door of return.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run a connector who pulls people together easily. The Ha that runs through the name points to an open, breath-like quality alive in conversation and feeling — someone who energises the people around them. The closing Dal suggests a person who leaves the door open — endings are rarely final.

The Letters

Reading Vahid through Ilm ul Huroof

Every letter in Vahid carries a role. Waw opens it outward; the letters between hold the interior; the last letter sets how things land.

وWawFirst · Outward SelfconnectionحHaInteriorlife-breathيYaInteriorreachingدDalLast · Resolutionreturn

Vahid opens with Waw (و) — connection, the divine conjunction. Then Ha (ح) — the breath of life, divine intimacy. Then Ya (ي) — reaching. Then Dal (د) — the returning door. Connection — breath of life — reaching — returning door. The unique: connected to the divine, breathing life with intimacy, always reaching, always returning through the door. True uniqueness is connected and returns.

The Portrait

What Vahid asks of its bearer

People named Vahid tend to have a genuinely original quality that cannot be replicated. The Waw connects them to the divine. The Ha breathes life and intimacy. The Ya reaches. The Dal close keeps returning: their uniqueness returns to its source.
