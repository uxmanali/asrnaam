Dictionary Meaning

What Al-Idrisi means

From the Idrīsid line, descendants of Idris ibn Abdullah, the founder of the first Moroccan dynasty. From the name Idrīs (إِدْريس), itself associated with the Quranic prophet (19:56). The suffix -ī marks descent.

Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Al-Idrisi.

  • Abū ʿAbdullāh Muḥammad ibn Muḥammad al-Sharīf al-Idrīsī (1100–1165 CE · Ceuta / Palermo (Sicily)). Patronised by Roger II of Sicily, Al-Idrisi spent fifteen years collating travellers' reports from every reachable coast and constructing the most accurate world map of the medieval period. His Kitāb Nuzhat al-Mushtāq (Book of Pleasant Journeys), known in Europe as the Tabula Rogeriana, divided the inhabited world into seven climes and seventy regions, with detailed descriptions of cities, trade routes, and physical geography from the Atlantic to China.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Al-Idrisi reveals

Al-Idrīsī opens with Alif (ا), origin, and closes on Ya (ي), reaching. Between them, Dāl (د), Rāʾ (ر), Sīn (س), door, mercy, horizon. A name shaped to map what is far.

Legacy

What Al-Idrisi left behind

The Tabula Rogeriana remained the most accurate world map in Europe and the Muslim world for three centuries, until Mercator. Al-Idrisi's silver planisphere (now lost) was a metre and a half across and engraved with the entire known world. Its descendant maps influenced every European cartographer through Columbus and beyond.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
