Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Al-Battani.

  • Abū ʿAbd Allāh Muḥammad ibn Jābir ibn Sinān al-Battānī al-Ḥarrānī al-Ṣābiʾ (c. 858 – 929 CE · Harran / Raqqa). Al-Battānī's Kitāb al-Zīj al-Ṣābiʾ revised Ptolemy's planetary tables with new observations made at Raqqa over decades. He determined the inclination of the ecliptic and the length of the solar year with significantly improved precision, identified the motion of the solar apogee, and gave more accurate values for the precession of the equinoxes. His tables circulated as Tabulae Sabaeae in Latin Europe and were used by Copernicus, who cited him repeatedly.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Al-Battani reveals

Al-Battānī opens with Ba (ب), the container, the holding vessel, apt for an astronomer whose tables held the motion of the heavens; through completion, origin, depth, to the self.

Legacy

What Al-Battani left behind

Al-Battānī's astronomical constants were the most accurate in the Mediterranean world for several centuries. The Sabian Zij was translated into Latin by Plato of Tivoli in the twelfth century as De Motu Stellarum and remained influential in European astronomy through Copernicus.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
