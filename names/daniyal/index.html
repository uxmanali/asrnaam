Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Daniyal reveals

Dal opens the door of divine judgement, Alif rises as upright origin, Nun closes with the knowledge of dreams, Ya carries his yearning toward the unseen, the second Alif anchors him again, and Lam binds him to the divine name. The letters together trace an opening, origin, knowing, yearning, and binding to the One.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run open-doored and receptive in how they greet people. The Alif that runs through the name points to a strong sense of self and a tendency to begin things — someone who draws others toward them as a starting point. The closing Lam suggests a person who shapes endings through love and attachment.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

Dictionary Meaning

What Daniyal means

Daniyāl (دانيال) is the Arabic-Urdu form of the Hebrew name דָּנִיֵּאל (Dānīʾēl), meaning 'God is my judge,' from the Hebrew dīn (judgement) and ʾēl (God). The figure of Daniel is not named in the Qurʾān but features prominently in classical Islamic tradition (qiṣaṣ al-anbiyāʾ) as a prophet of the post-exilic period who interpreted the dreams of the Babylonian king Bukhtnaṣar (Nebuchadnezzar) and survived being thrown into the lions' den. The early Muslim conquest of Persia in the seventh century is traditionally said to have rediscovered his preserved body in Susa.

Daniyal has been one of the most popular masculine names in the Pakistani-British community over the past two decades, consistently among the top fifty boys' names in UK Pakistani families. It is also widely used across South Asia, Iran, Turkey (as Daniyal/Danyal), and Indonesia. Notable bearers include Daniyal Mueenuddin, the Pakistani-American novelist (In Other Rooms, Other Wonders), and Daniyal Aziz, the Pakistani politician.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Daniyal

د-ن-ي-ل — Arabized form of Hebrew Dāniyyēl ('God is my judge'). Classical Islamic tradition recognises Dānyāl as a wise believer of the Babylonian period; his story is told in detail by Tabari and Ibn Kathir though he is not named in the Qur'an.

The Portrait

What Daniyal asks of its bearer

The name opens with the long Alif (د is preceded by Dāl), travels through Nūn (ن) and Yāʾ (ي), and closes through Lām. A bearer of Daniyal is named into the prophetic tradition of dream interpretation — the gift of seeing meaning where others see only image.
