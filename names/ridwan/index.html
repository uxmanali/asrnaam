Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

ر-ض-و

ر-ض-و, from r-ḍ-y, divine pleasure. Riḍwān names the divine well-pleasure greater than all the gardens of Paradise (Q 9:72). Riḍwān is also the name of the angel guardian of Paradise.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Ridwan is grounded in earth, the element of stability, patience, and quiet holding. The letters ask their bearer to be reliable rather than showy, to keep the household of the heart in order. Stitched through this reading is Al-Rahman, the Merciful, meaning the person carries mercy that keeps moving even when tested. A person named Ridwan is the household others rest against without noticing they are resting.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

Divine pleasure, contentment, directly Quranic. 'The pleasure of Allah (ridwan Allah) is greater' (9:72). 'Allah is pleased with them and they are pleased with Him' (9:100). 'Seeking the pleasure of Allah (mardhat Allah)' (2:207). Ridwan names the man who lives for divine pleasure, who measures every action by whether Allah is pleased with it. The ultimate quality.

Ridwan is also the name of the angel who guards paradise.
