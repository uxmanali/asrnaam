Dictionary Meaning

What Barakah means

Blessing — Barakah bint Tha'laba (RA), known as Umm Ayman, was the nursemaid and second mother of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. She served his family before and after prophethood. The Prophet said: 'Whoever wishes to marry a woman from the people of paradise, let him marry Umm Ayman.' After the Prophet's death she wept, saying: 'I knew the Prophet would die — I weep because revelation has ended.' She lived to after the death of Umar (RA).

The Letters

Reading Barakah through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

Ba (vessel) — Ra (perpetual mercy) — Kaf (sufficiency) — Ta (manifestation). Blessing: a vessel for mercy, perpetually merciful, sufficient, manifest.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

About the name Barakah

What does the name Barakah mean?
Barakah means: Blessing — Umm Ayman, nursemaid of the Prophet, called his mother. Umm Ayman — nursemaid of the Prophet. 'Whoever wishes to marry from the people of paradise, let him marry Umm Ayman.' The woman the Prophet called mother.
Is Barakah mentioned in the Quran?
'And We have blessed him (barakna alayhi)' (37:113 — of Ibrahim). 'Blessed is He (tabarak)' — the most common Quranic phrase of divine praise. Barakah carries this quality of divine blessing.
What does Barakah mean in Urdu?

Is Barakah a good Islamic name?
Yes, Barakah is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: Blessing — Umm Ayman, nursemaid of the Prophet, called his mother. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
Is Barakah a girl or boy name?
Barakah is traditionally a Muslim girl name meaning Blessing — Umm Ayman, nursemaid of the Prophet, called his mother.
What is the spiritual meaning of Barakah in Ilm ul Huroof?
Ba holds as vessel, Ra moves with mercy, Kaf gives sufficiency, Ta manifests. Blessing: vessel, merciful, sufficient, manifest.
