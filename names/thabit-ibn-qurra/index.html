Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Abū al-Ḥasan Thābit ibn Qurra ibn Marwān al-Ṣābiʾ al-Ḥarrānī (c. 826 – 901 CE · Harran / Baghdad). Thābit was a Sabian from Harran brought to Baghdad by the Banū Mūsā brothers. He revised and translated Greek mathematical and astronomical works, including Archimedes, Apollonius, Euclid, and Ptolemy, into Arabic, often improving them. His original mathematical contributions include a generalisation of the Pythagorean theorem, a method for finding amicable numbers, and work on parabolic and paraboloid volumes that anticipates integration.

Yes. Thabit ibn Qurra is a recognised Arabic name of significant historical weight in the Muslim world, borne by The Translator-Geometer.
