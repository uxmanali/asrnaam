Dictionary Meaning

What Ibn Zuhr means

Son of Zuhr — light, brightness, the flowering form. From ز-ه-ر (z-h-r) — to shine, to bloom; same root as زُهرَة (zuhra, flower; Venus). The female name Zuhra shares this root.

Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Ibn Zuhr.

  • Abū Marwān ʿAbd al-Malik ibn Zuhr (c. 1094–1162 CE · Seville (al-Andalus)) — Known in Latin as Avenzoar, Ibn Zuhr was the most accomplished clinical physician of the Western Islamic world. His Kitāb al-Taysīr fī al-Mudāwāt wa-l-Tadbīr (Book of Simplification on Therapy and Regimen) — written at the request of his friend Ibn Rushd — became, in Latin translation, a standard medical text in European universities for four centuries. He described pericarditis, mediastinal abscesses, and was the first to advocate experimentation on animals before applying treatment to humans.

Legacy

What Ibn Zuhr left behind

Ibn Zuhr's clinical method — observe, infer, then treat — laid the basis for evidence-based medical practice. His advocacy of animal experimentation before human treatment anticipates modern pharmacological ethics by eight centuries. Ibn Rushd, his close friend, names him repeatedly as the finest physician since Galen.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
