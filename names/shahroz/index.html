Dictionary Meaning

What Shahroz means

King of the day — from Persian شهروز (Shahroz — king of the day, shah of the day). Shah (king/sovereign) + roz (day) = the king of the day, the one who rules with the brilliance and openness of daylight. The name carries the quality of the man whose sovereignty is as clear, warm, and open as the day itself — transparent, brilliant, and beneficent.

Shahroz is trending strongly in Pakistan and among South Asian Muslim communities in 2026 — a name of kingly, daylight quality appealing to parents who want something distinctive and powerful.

PronunciationSHAH-roz  ·  shah·roz  ·  2 syllables

Frequently Asked Questions

About the name Shahroz

What does the name Shahroz mean?
Shahroz means: King of the day — the sovereign who rules with the brilliance of daylight. From Persian shah (king) + roz (day). King of the day — the sovereign whose authority is as open and brilliant as daylight. Trending Pakistan and South Asian Muslim communities 2026.
Is Shahroz mentioned in the Quran?
The day (yawm/nahar) is a Quranic sign: 'And the day when He displays it' (91:3). 'And We made the day for livelihood' (78:11). The king (shah) and the day (roz) together carry the Quranic qualities of sovereignty and life-giving brightness.

Is Shahroz a good Islamic name?
Yes, Shahroz is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: King of the day — the sovereign who rules with the brilliance of daylight. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
