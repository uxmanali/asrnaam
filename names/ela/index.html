Dictionary Meaning

What Ela means

Hazel, hazel-eyed, light — from Turkish إيلا (ela — hazel colour, the warm light-brown colour of hazel eyes). One of the most beloved names in Turkey for its simplicity and its connection to the most celebrated eye colour in Turkish poetic tradition — the ela (hazel) eyes of the beloved are praised in countless classical Ottoman poems.

Ela also connects to Hebrew Elah (terebinth tree, the tree of strength) and is used as a variant of Aliya (elevated) in some traditions. The name carries the quality of warm, natural luminosity — the colour that is between brown and green, between earth and sky, the colour that contains all colours.

The Letters

Reading Ela through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior life. The final letter governs how things resolve. For Turkish, Persian and Urdu names, the reading draws on the nearest Arabic letter equivalents.

اAlifFirst · Outward SelfلLamInteriorاAlifLast · Resolution

Ela is a three-letter name: Alif (ا), Lam (ل), Alif (ا). Two Alifs frame the Lam. Origin — divine authority — origin. The hazel-eyed: beginning in origin, holding divine authority between the two origins, returning. Two origins with divine authority between them — the simplest possible expression of what is elevated and genuine.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Ela asks of its bearer

People named Ela tend to have a quality of warm, natural attractiveness that does not depend on any particular feature — there is something about them that is simply pleasant to be near. The framing Alifs give them a quality of completion and return. The Lam between them gives divine authority: their warmth and colour have an authoritative quality. Simple and complete.

A Notable Bearer

The hazel (ela) eye is the most celebrated colour in classical Ottoman Turkish poetry — the eye that contains all colours, that is warm without being common, that is natural without being ordinary. Ela names the girl who carries this quality of warm, complete natural beauty.
