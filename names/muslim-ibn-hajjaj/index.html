Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Muslim ibn Hajjaj.

  • Abū al-Ḥusayn Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj ibn Muslim al-Qushayrī al-Naysābūrī (c. 815–875 CE · Nishapur (Khurasan)). Imām Muslim compiled al-Jāmiʿ al-Ṣaḥīḥ, known as Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, the second of the two most authoritative collections of prophetic hadith in Sunni Islam. He gathered some 7,500 hadith narrations (about 4,000 unique reports) using rigorous transmission criteria slightly different from al-Bukhārī's, and he is particularly admired for the clarity of his organisation: he groups all narrations of a single hadith in one place rather than dispersing them across legal chapters.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Muslim ibn Hajjaj reveals

Muslim opens with love (Mīm) and the secret (Sīn), threads through divine binding (Lām), and closes in love again. A name doubled in love and bound at its centre, a structure fitting for a scholar whose life-work was to preserve the words of the Beloved (the Prophet ﷺ) with utmost care.

Legacy

What Muslim ibn Hajjaj left behind

Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim is held by Sunni scholarly consensus to rank immediately after Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī in authenticity. His introduction to the work is itself a foundational treatise on the methodology of hadith criticism, and his careful preservation of variant chains of transmission gives the work an enduring value for scholarly research.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
