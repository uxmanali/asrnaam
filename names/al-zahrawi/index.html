Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Al-Zahrawi.

  • Abū al-Qāsim Khalaf ibn ʿAbbās al-Zahrāwī (c. 936 – 1013 CE · Cordoba / al-Zahrāʾ (al-Andalus)). Al-Zahrāwī served as physician at the Umayyad court in Cordoba. His Kitāb al-Taṣrīf li-man ʿAjiza ʿan al-Taʾlīf is a thirty-volume medical encyclopedia whose last book is the first illustrated surgical treatise, containing detailed drawings of more than 200 surgical instruments he designed or refined. He covered cauterisation, cataract surgery, lithotripsy, obstetrics, dentistry, and the suture using catgut. His descriptions remained the surgical standard in both the Arabic and Latin medical worlds for five centuries.

Legacy

What Al-Zahrawi left behind

The surgical book of the Taṣrīf was translated into Latin by Gerard of Cremona in the twelfth century and printed regularly into the seventeenth. The illustrated approach he pioneered shaped European surgical pedagogy. Several of his instruments, modified versions of his vaginal speculum, urethral sound, and obstetric forceps, remained in use into the modern era.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
