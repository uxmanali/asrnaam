Dictionary Meaning

What Esma means

Names, lofty qualities — Turkish form of أسماء (Asma — names, the one of high qualities). The plural of اسم (name). Allah taught Adam all the names — the names are the divine knowledge of realities. Asma bint Abi Bakr (RA) was the daughter of Abu Bakr al-Siddiq and the sister of Aisha (RA), known as Dhat al-Nitaqayn (She of the Two Belts) for cutting her belt in two to carry food secretly to the Prophet ﷺ and her father in the cave of Thawr during the Hijra.

Esma is one of the most common girls' names in Turkey and across the Balkans, beloved for its connection to this heroic companion and for its meaning of the one who holds many excellent qualities.

The Letters

Reading Esma through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior life. The final letter governs how things resolve. For Turkish, Persian and Urdu names, the reading draws on the nearest Arabic letter equivalents.

Esma opens and closes with Alif (ا) — a name framed by origin and return. Within: Sin (س) — flowing expansion — and Meem (م) — love, depth. Alif — flowing expansion — love and depth — Alif. The woman of many names: she begins in origin, flows outward, holds love and depth, and returns to origin. The double Alif makes her complete — she is a circle of excellence.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Esma asks of its bearer

People named Esma tend to have a quality of wholeness — they contain many things without contradiction. The framing Alifs give them completion. The Sin gives them a flowing, expansive quality. The Meem holds love and depth at the centre. Their excellence is not in one area but in many — they are named after names themselves, the multiplicity of divine attributes.

A Notable Bearer

Asma bint Abi Bakr (RA) — Dhat al-Nitaqayn (She of the Two Belts), who risked her life carrying food to the Prophet ﷺ and her father in the cave of Thawr. Daughter of the first Caliph, sister of Aisha, she lived to 100 and died shortly after her son Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr.
