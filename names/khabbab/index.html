Dictionary Meaning

What Khabbab means

Quick, lively — from root خ-ب-ب (khabbaba — to be quick, lively). خباب (Khabbab — quick, lively). Khabbab ibn al-Aratt (RA) was one of the earliest Muslims, enslaved to a Qurayshi woman. He was tortured repeatedly — hot coals were placed on his bare back until they were extinguished by the melting of his flesh. He went to the Prophet ﷺ and said: 'Will you not pray to Allah for us?' The Prophet said: 'The believers before you were tortured more severely and remained firm. Be patient.' He survived to see Islam's triumph and lived to 63.

The Letters

Reading Khabbab through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

خKhaFirst · Outward SelfبBaInteriorاAlifInteriorبBaLast · Resolution

Kha (kh) — hidden interior. Ba (b) — vessel. Alif (a) — origin. Ba (b) — vessel again. Hidden interior — vessel — origin — vessel. The firm: from hidden interior depth, doubly a vessel for endurance, originary. Double Ba: doubly holding what cannot be broken.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Khabbab asks of its bearer

People named Khabbab carry a quality of deep, hidden, doubly-holding endurance — they cannot be broken.

A Notable Bearer

Khabbab ibn al-Aratt (RA) — hot coals placed on his back, extinguished by melting flesh. He endured. The Prophet's words to him: 'Be patient' — became one of the most cited prophetic counsels.

Frequently Asked Questions

About the name Khabbab

What does the name Khabbab mean?
Khabbab means: Quick, lively — the companion tortured with burning coals who remained firm. One of the earliest Muslims, tortured with burning coals. 'The believers before you were tortured more severely and remained firm' — the Prophet to Khabbab. Model of unbreakable early faith.
Is Khabbab mentioned in the Quran?
'Indeed the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous' (49:13). Khabbab's endurance under torture is the model of the early believer's firmness.
What does Khabbab mean in Urdu?
Khabbab in Urdu is خباب (Khabbab) meaning تیز رفتار، زندہ — quick, lively, the one who remained firm.
Is Khabbab a good Islamic name?
Yes, Khabbab is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: Quick, lively — the companion tortured with burning coals who remained firm. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
Is Khabbab a boy or girl name?
Khabbab is traditionally a Muslim boy name meaning Quick, lively — the companion tortured with burning coals who remained firm.
What is the spiritual meaning of Khabbab in Ilm ul Huroof?
Kha gives hidden depth, double Ba holds doubly, Alif grounds. The firm: from deep within, doubly holding, originary.
