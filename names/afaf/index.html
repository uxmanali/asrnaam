Dictionary Meaning

What Afaf means

From the Arabic ʿafāf (عفاف) — chastity, modesty, the inner integrity that does not yield. The root ʿ-f-f yields ʿiffah (chastity, abstinence from what is forbidden), ʿafīf (m, chaste), and ʿafīfa (f, chaste). The Quran uses related vocabulary in praising the women who guard themselves: wa-l-mukhṣināti mina al-muʾmināt — 'the chaste women among the believers' (5:5).

A widely-used girls' name across the Arab world, especially among Egyptian, Levantine, and Maghrebi families. Bearer notes: Afaf Radi, Egyptian singer; Afaf el-Sayyed, Egyptian-American historian.

Variants: Afaaf, Afaf, Afāf. Same chastity-family: Afifa, Afeefah, Iffat (Persian/Urdu form). The masculine form is Afif. Pair-names in Egyptian families: Aisha, Khadija, Maryam, Salma.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Afaf

ع-ف-ف — From ʿ-f-f, chastity, modesty, restraint — ʿafāf is purity of conduct, ʿafīf the chaste one. Same root as iʿtifāf (self-restraint).

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Afaf reveals

Ain perceives the source, Fa is opening, Alif stands as the One, Fa opens again. Perception followed by a steady opening and closing around the upright Alif — modesty as a hinged, watchful door.

The Portrait

What Afaf asks of its bearer

The first letter of Afaf is Ain — an outward self that sees deeply. Inside the name, Fa brings an interior that articulates; Alif brings an originary axis at the interior. These are what the name does without ever announcing it. Afaf ends in Fa — the life closes in articulation. The work of the name is to let chastity, modesty live through every letter — so that her life takes the same shape as her name.
