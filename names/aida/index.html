Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

ع-و-د

ع-و-د. From ʿ-w-d, to return, visit. ʿĀʾida is the active participle, she who returns or visits, sharing the root with ʿīd (festival of return).

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Aida weaves earth and water, the elements of steadiness and mercy, patience and feeling. The person is asked to be both root and river, a slow, sheltering presence. Running through the reading is Al-Alim, the All-Knowing, meaning the bearer is trusted with a knowing that predates study, joined by Al-Tawwab, the Relenting, carried as the readiness to return and be returned to. A person named Aida is the family's slow heart.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

Returning, the visiting one, from root ain-w-d (ada, to return, to visit). Aida names the girl who returns, to good, to Allah, to home. Top Arab and Bosnian girls name. Verdi's opera Aida is set in the Islamic world and carries the name to global recognition.
