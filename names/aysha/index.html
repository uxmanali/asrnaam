What Aysha reveals

Meaning

What Aysha means

Aysha means: Living, happy — a simplified spelling used worldwide. The name comes from the Arabic عائشة and carries the same deep Islamic meaning regardless of which spelling is used.

Aysha is a simplified variant of Aisha/Ayesha, used widely in Turkish, Bosnian, Albanian communities and across the Muslim diaspora in the West. Three letters, clean and clear — same name, same meaning, the same brilliant, living, joyful woman who was the Prophet's ﷺ most beloved wife.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Aysha

ع-ي-ش — From ʿ-y-sh, to live — the same root as ʿaysh (life). ʿĀʾisha is the active participle: 'she who lives, full of life'.

Frequently Asked Questions

About the name Aysha

What does the name Aysha mean?
Aysha means: Living, happy — a simplified spelling used worldwide. It is a spelling variant of Aisha — different English transliteration of the same Arabic name عائشة.
Is Aysha the same as Aisha?
Yes — Aysha and Aisha are different English spellings of the same Arabic name. They carry identical meaning and identical Islamic heritage.
Is Aysha a good Islamic name?
Yes, Aysha is an excellent Islamic name. It is a valid transliteration of Aisha (عائشة) — Living, happy — a simplified spelling used worldwide.
Which spelling is correct — Aysha or Aisha?
Both are correct. Arabic names can be spelled multiple ways in English because English does not have a standardised system for transliterating Arabic letters. The Arabic name عائشة is the same regardless of English spelling. Use whichever spelling your community and family prefers.
What is the Ilm ul Huroof reading of Aysha?
The Ilm ul Huroof reading is based on the Arabic letters of the name, not the English spelling. See the complete letter-by-letter spiritual reading on the Aisha page.
