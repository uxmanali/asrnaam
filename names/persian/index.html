Persian-Origin Muslim Names — Iranian, Pashto & Persianate Tradition

Names rooted in the Iranian, Pashto, and broader Persianate world — carrying Persian poetic imagery (the moon, the rose, the dawn) alongside the Islamic register inherited from the Quran.

67 names

Persian Muslim naming spans a vast geography — Iran, Afghanistan, Tajikistan, parts of Pakistan, Central Asia, and the Mughal courts of the Indian subcontinent where Persian was the language of administration and culture for over three centuries. The vocabulary that shapes the names is shared: nāz (grace), mehr (kindness, the sun), gul (the flower), māh (the moon), shīrīn (sweet), sitāra (a star), parvāneh (a butterfly), pari (a fairy).

Inside this poetic register the Islamic content is fully present — Persian forms of Quranic names (Ebrahim, Eisa, Mostafa, Hossein, Reza), divine titles arabised back through Persian (Mehdi, Mohsen, Morteza), and Sufi vocabulary woven into proper names (Reza — divine pleasure; Vahid — singular). Frequently Asked Questions

What are Persian Muslim names?

Persian Muslim names are names rooted in the Iranian, Afghan, Tajik, and broader Persianate cultural world. They include Persian poetic vocabulary (Shirin — sweet, Bahar — spring, Niloofar — lotus) alongside Persianised forms of Quranic names (Ebrahim, Eisa, Hossein, Mostafa, Reza).

Are Persian names Islamic?

Persian Muslim names are fully Islamic in tradition. The Persianate world has been Muslim since the 7th century, and Persian was the courtly language of Mughal India for over three centuries. Many names blend the Islamic register (Mehdi, Mohsen, Reza) with Persian poetic imagery (Mehr — kindness/sun, Gulnaz — flower-grace).

What is a popular Persian girl name?

Mahsa (like the moon), Niloofar (lotus), Yasaman (jasmine), Shirin (sweet), Nazanin (darling), and Laleh (tulip — Iran's national flower) are among the most-loved Iranian girl names today.

What is a popular Persian boy name?

Reza (divine pleasure), Mohsen (doer of good), Mehdi (the guided one), Hossein, Ali, Milad (birth), and Arash (the legendary archer) are common Iranian boy names — blending Islamic and pre-Islamic Persian heritage.
