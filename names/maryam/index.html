Dictionary Meaning

What Maryam means

Mary — from Hebrew Miriam (beloved, exalted). Maryam bint Imran (AS) is the only woman mentioned by name in the Quran, and she has an entire surah named after her — Surah Maryam (Chapter 19). The Quran says Allah chose her above all women: 'O Maryam! Allah has chosen you, purified you, and chosen you above the women of all worlds' (3:42).

She conceived Eisa (AS) without a father by Allah's direct command. When accused by her people, she pointed to the infant, who then spoke from the cradle. She is called Al-Batool (the Pure) and Al-Siddiqah (the Truthful).

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Maryam reveals

Meem love, Ra mercy, Ya reaching, Alif origin, Meem love. Two Meems frame the name with love; mercy and reaching to origin between — a mother's pattern.

How This Shows Up in Life

Personality & relational pattern

People named Maryam tend to be soft and loving, instinctively familial in their bearing. The opening Meem carries the pattern inward — a deep capacity for love and a strong tie to family and inner faith, and someone who tends to become the emotional centre of the people around them. The repeated Meem at both ends suggests a person who completes things in love — few endings are bitter.

An interpretive reading drawn from the letters of the name — for reflection, not prediction.

The Portrait

What Maryam asks of its bearer

People named Maryam tend to have a quality of chosen purity — something about them is genuinely untouched and genuinely devoted. The double Meem gives double love. The Ra moves with perpetual mercy. The Ya reaches. Their quality is framed entirely in love.

A Notable Bearer

Maryam bint Imran (AS) — the only woman named in the Quran, chosen above all women of all worlds. Mother of Eisa (AS). The Quran devotes more space to her story than the Bible does. Surah Maryam (Chapter 19) bears her name.
