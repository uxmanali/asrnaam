Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

Mary, from Hebrew Miriam (beloved, exalted). Maryam bint Imran (AS) is the only woman mentioned by name in the Quran, and she has an entire surah named after her. Surah Maryam (Chapter 19). The Quran says Allah chose her above all women: 'O Maryam! Allah has chosen you, purified you, and chosen you above the women of all worlds' (3:42).

She conceived Eisa (AS) without a father by Allah's direct command. When accused by her people, she pointed to the infant, who then spoke from the cradle. She is called Al-Batool (the Pure) and Al-Siddiqah (the Truthful).

Maryam (AS) is the only woman named directly in the Quran. Surah Maryam (Chapter 19) bears her name; her story spans multiple surahs, Āl ʿImrān (3:35–47), al-Nisāʾ (4:156–171), al-Mā'ida (5:75), and others. The Quran says: 'O Maryam, indeed Allah has chosen you and purified you and chosen you above the women of the worlds' (3:42).

Her conception of ʿĪsā (AS) without a father is described in 19:16–22 and 21:91. She is called al-Ṣiddīqa (the Truthful, 5:75) and al-Qānita (the devoutly obedient, 66:12). No other woman receives this much Quranic space.

Maryam means: Mary, the only woman named in the Quran, mother of Eisa. In Islamic tradition, this name carries deep significance rooted in Arabic etymology and spiritual heritage.
