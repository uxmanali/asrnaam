Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Ibn al-Nafis.

  • ʿAlāʾ al-Dīn Abū al-Ḥasan ʿAlī ibn Abī al-Ḥazm al-Qurashī al-Dimashqī (1213 – 1288 CE · Damascus / Cairo). Ibn al-Nafīs was the chief physician at the Manṣūrī hospital in Cairo. In his Sharḥ Tashrīḥ al-Qānūn, a commentary on the anatomical section of Ibn Sīnā's Canon, he gave the first known description of pulmonary circulation, asserting that blood passes from the right ventricle through the lungs to the left ventricle, against Galen's account of a porous interventricular septum. The discovery anticipates Servetus (1553) and Harvey (1628) by centuries. He also wrote the encyclopedic al-Shāmil fī al-Ṣināʿa al-Ṭibbiyya and works on hadith and Shāfiʿī law.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Ibn al-Nafis reveals

Ibn al-Nafīs opens with Alif and closes on Sin (س), the horizon, the secret within, fitting for a physician whose great discovery lay in observation no one else had recorded.

Legacy

What Ibn al-Nafis left behind

His work was rediscovered only in the twentieth century, the manuscript of the Sharḥ lay unread in libraries, and the priority claim for pulmonary circulation now rests securely with him in the history of medicine.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
