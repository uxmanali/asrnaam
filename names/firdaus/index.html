Dictionary Meaning

What Firdaus means

Firdaus (فردوس, firdaws) is the Qur'anic word for the highest paradise — the supreme garden of Eden reserved for the foremost of the righteous. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ taught: "When you ask Allah, ask Him for al-Firdaws, for it is the highest part of Paradise and the middle of Paradise, and from it spring the rivers of Paradise" (Bukhārī). The word appears twice in the Qur'an: in 18:107 (jannāt al-firdaws) as the reward of those who believe and do righteous deeds, and in 23:11 where the inheritors of Firdaws are described as those who guard their prayers and turn away from idle speech. The word is of ancient Persian/Avestan origin (pairi-daēza, "enclosed garden"), entering Arabic through pre-Islamic exchange. Firdaus is used as both a masculine and a feminine name across the Muslim world — most commonly feminine in South Asia (Pakistan, Bangladesh, India) and as Firdous/Firdaws in the Arab world. Variants include Firdous, Ferdaus, and the Persian Ferdows.
