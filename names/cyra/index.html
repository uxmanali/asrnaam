Dictionary Meaning

What Cyra means

Sun, throne — from Persian سیرا (Cyra — sun, throne, the one of royal dignity and light). The feminine form of Cyrus. In Persian tradition, the name carries the dual quality of solar light and royal authority — the woman of radiant dignity whose presence commands the space around her.

Cyra has become popular in Western Muslim communities as a feminine name of Persian heritage — elegant in sound, meaningful in its connection to Persian Islamic civilisation, and carrying the quality of the woman of light and authority.

The Letters

Reading Cyra through Ilm ul Huroof

Every name is a sequence of principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior life. The final letter governs how things resolve. For Persian, Turkish and Urdu names, the reading draws on the nearest Arabic letter equivalents.

Cyra maps to سیرا: Sin (س) — the flowing expansion. Ya (ي) — the reaching hand. Ra (ر) — mercy in perpetual motion. Alif (ا) — origin, return. Flowing expansion (Sin) — reaching (Ya) — mercy in motion (Ra) — returning to origin (Alif). The solar woman: flowing and expanding, always reaching, moving with mercy, and always returning to her origin. The sun rises and sets — it always returns.

The Portrait

What Cyra asks of its bearer

People named Cyra tend to have a quality of dignified radiance — they illuminate the space they occupy without announcing it. The Sin gives them flowing expansion: their quality spreads. The Ya reaches. The Ra moves with mercy. The Alif close returns them to origin: their light comes from somewhere real, and they always come back to it.

A Notable Bearer

Cyra — the feminine form of Cyrus, the great Persian king. Persian civilisation produced some of the greatest contributions to Islamic culture: poetry, architecture, mathematics, philosophy. Cyra carries this heritage of light and dignity.
