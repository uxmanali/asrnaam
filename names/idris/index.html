إدريس
Male Muslim Name  ·  Ilm ul Huroof Reading

Idris — The one who studies, who is assiduous in learning

What the name Idris means, what its letters say, and what it asks of the person who carries it.

Dictionary Meaning

What Idris means

The one who studies, who is assiduous in learning — from root د-ر-س (darasa — to study, to teach, to efface through repetition). Idris is the one for whom learning is a constitutive quality.

Root د-ر-س: Study, teaching, the repeated engagement that wears a path through knowledge. دراسة (dirasa — study), مدرسة (madrasa — school, the place of studying). The root suggests knowledge gained through devoted, repeated engagement rather than sudden insight.

The Letters

Reading Idris through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self — the primary mode through which the person meets the world. The middle letters govern the interior life. The final letter governs how things resolve.

ا Alif First · Outward Self د Dal Interior ر Ra Interior ي Ya Interior س Seen Last · Resolution

Idris opens with Alif — origin, first principle — and closes with Seen — the horizon, continuous forward flow, the sirr. The name moves from origin to horizon with Dal (guidance), Ra (continuous movement), and Ya (the reaching hand) as the journey. A name that begins from first principle and ends always at the next horizon — the eternal student.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi
The Portrait

What Idris asks of its bearer

People named Idris carry the study principle — they tend to be people for whom learning never stops, who find new depths in things others have finished with. The Alif opening means they approach knowledge from first principles; they want to understand the foundation, not just the application. The Seen close means they are always at the horizon — there is always one more thing to understand.

A Notable Bearer

Idris (AS), the Prophet, mentioned twice in the Quran, described as truthful and patient. Traditionally identified with Enoch. The one who was raised to a high station.
