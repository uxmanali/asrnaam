Dictionary Meaning

What Hamza means

From the Arabic ḥamza (حمزة) — 'lion'. Classical Arabic poetry uses ḥamza as the praise-epithet for the warrior at the height of his strength. The most weight-bearing companion-bearer is Ḥamzah ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib (d. 625 CE) — the uncle of the Prophet ﷺ, called Asadullāh ('the Lion of Allah') and Sayyid al-Shuhadāʾ ('the Master of Martyrs'). He was martyred at the battle of Uhud (625 CE), and the Prophet ﷺ wept over his body.

One of the most-used boys' names across the Muslim world: Pakistan, Egypt, Turkey, Indonesia, the Maghreb, the Levant. Bearer notes: Hamza Yusuf (born 1958), American Islamic scholar; Hamza Ali Abbasi, Pakistani actor.

Variants: Hamzah, Hamzeh (Levantine), Hamzi, Hamze (Turkish). Same lion-family: Asad, Haidar, Shir, Arsalan. Pair-names: Bilal, Salman, Ali, Umar — the early Companion names. Compound: Hamza ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib (the full traditional Companion-name).

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Hamza

ح-م-ز — From ḥ-m-z, to be steadfast and sharp; the lion association comes from this strength.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Hamza reveals

Ḥa breath, Meem love, Zayn adornment, Alif origin. A strength that breathes, loves, is beautifully arrayed, and returns to origin — not the lion of fury but the lion of dignity.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run warm and alive in conversation. The Meem that runs through the name points to a deep capacity for love and a strong tie to family and inner faith — someone who tends to become the emotional centre of the people around them. The closing Ta suggests a person who completes things cleanly, with few loose ends.

The Portrait

What Hamza asks of its bearer

People named Hamza have a quality of protective strength. The Ha breathes life. The Meem gives love as the fuel of their protection. The Zain adorns. The Ha close breathes divine presence: their lion quality comes from somewhere divine.

A Notable Bearer

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the name Hamza mean?

Hamza means: Lion — the strongest companion, the Master of Martyrs. In Islamic tradition, this name carries deep significance rooted in Arabic etymology and spiritual heritage.

Is Hamza a good Islamic name?

Yes, Hamza is an excellent Islamic name. The Prophet ﷺ encouraged giving children beautiful names with good meanings. Hamza carries lion — the strongest companion, the master of martyrs, making it a blessed and meaningful choice.

Is Hamza mentioned in the Quran?

No. Hamza is not directly mentioned in the Quran by name, but the name carries deep Islamic meaning and is widely used in Muslim communities.
