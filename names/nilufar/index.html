Dictionary Meaning

What Nilufar means

Lotus flower — from Persian نيلوفر (Nilufar — the lotus flower, the water lily). The lotus in Persian and Central Asian tradition is the supreme symbol of pure beauty — it grows from muddy water yet emerges spotlessly pure and radiant, floating above what would diminish.

Nilufar is one of the most popular Muslim girl names in Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and across the Persian-speaking world. In Germany and Europe, it is carried by hundreds of thousands of Central Asian Muslim women. The Uzbek and Tajik diaspora make this a highly searched name globally. The name carries the quality of the woman who rises pure and beautiful from whatever circumstances she emerged from.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Nilufar

Persian origin — nilūfar, water lily, lotus. A Central Asian girls' name across Uzbekistan, Tajikistan.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Nilufar reveals

Nun of depth opens, Ya of reaching, Lam of authority, Waw of connection, Fa of opening, Ra of mercy. The lotus that the soul resembles.

How This Shows Up in Life

Personality & relational pattern

People named Nilufar tend to be watchful, inward, present in a quiet way. The Ya that runs through the name points to a reaching, aspirational interior — someone who draws people upward toward what they're moving toward. The closing Ra suggests a person who lets endings push into the next beginning.
