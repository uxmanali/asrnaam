Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Laila

ل-ي-ل — From l-y-l (night). Layla: 'of the night' — but in classical Arab usage also 'dark-skinned beauty, intoxicating'. The udhrī beloved of Qays/Majnūn fixed the name forever.

Meaning

What Laila means

Laila means: Night — the most common alternative spelling globally. The name comes from the Arabic ليلى and carries the same deep Islamic meaning regardless of which spelling is used.

Laila is one of the most popular spellings globally — used across Scandinavia (Norway has thousands of women named Laila), Germany, Turkey, and South Asia. Laila and Layla are the same Arabic name ليلى — the night, the beloved of Qais/Majnun, the most celebrated name in all of Arabic and Persian love poetry.

Frequently Asked Questions

About the name Laila

What does the name Laila mean?
Laila means: Night — the most common alternative spelling globally. It is a spelling variant of Layla — different English transliteration of the same Arabic name ليلى.
Is Laila the same as Layla?
Yes — Laila and Layla are different English spellings of the same Arabic name. They carry identical meaning and identical Islamic heritage.
Is Laila a good Islamic name?
Yes, Laila is an excellent Islamic name. It is a valid transliteration of Layla (ليلى) — Night — the most common alternative spelling globally.
Which spelling is correct — Laila or Layla?
Both are correct. Arabic names can be spelled multiple ways in English because English does not have a standardised system for transliterating Arabic letters. The Arabic name ليلى is the same regardless of English spelling. Use whichever spelling your community and family prefers.
