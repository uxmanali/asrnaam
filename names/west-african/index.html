West African Muslim Names, Senegal, Mali, Nigeria & the Sahel

Mande, Wolof, Fula, Hausa, Mandinka, and Soninké forms of Muslim names from West Africa, the spellings actually used across Senegal, Mali, Niger, Mauritania, Guinea, Nigeria, and Ghana.

26 names

The Muslim names of West Africa are the result of more than a millennium of Islamic scholarship across the Sahel. From the Ghanaian and Malian empires through the Sokoto Caliphate, through the Tijāniyya and Qādiriyya Sufi orders that remain influential today, the names of the Prophet ﷺ, his family, and the Quran's vocabulary have been carried in spellings that fit local languages: Wolof, Mandinka, Bambara, Fula, Hausa, Soninké.

Mamadou (Muhammad), Ibrahima (Ibrahim), Aboubakry (Abū Bakr), Mariama (Maryam), Aichatou (ʿĀʾisha), Khadijatou (Khadīja), Fatoumata (Fāṭima), each is a settled canonical spelling in the communities that use it. The meaning, the Arabic root, and the Ilm ul Huroof letter-reading of the underlying Arabic name remain unchanged; the spelling reflects the language of the family.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are common West African Muslim names?

Common West African Muslim names include Mamadou (Muhammad), Ibrahima (Ibrahim), Aboubacar (Abu Bakr), Mariama (Maryam), Fatoumata (Fatima), Aichatou (Aisha), Khadijatou (Khadija), and Aminata (Amina). The spellings reflect Wolof, Mandinka, Bambara, Fula, Hausa, and Soninké phonology.

Why do West African Muslim names look different from Arabic names?

Over more than a thousand years of Islamic scholarship in the Sahel, from the Ghanaian and Malian empires through the Sokoto Caliphate to the Tijāniyya and Qādiriyya Sufi orders, Arabic Muslim names settled into local languages with their own canonical spellings. The Arabic meaning, root, and Ilm ul Huroof reading remain unchanged.

What is the most popular Muslim name in Senegal?

Mamadou (the Senegalese form of Muhammad) has been the most popular boy name in Senegal for generations. Mouhamadou and Modou are also common Senegalese spellings of the same name. For girls, Aïssatou (Aisha) and Fatou (Fatima) lead.

Are West African Muslim names traditional?

Yes. These names follow a continuous Islamic naming tradition in West Africa dating to the spread of Islam in the region from the 8th century. The forms used in Senegal, Mali, Niger, Mauritania, Guinea, Nigeria, and Ghana are recognised settled spellings, not modern inventions.
