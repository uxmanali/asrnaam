What Tanveer means

Illumination, the giving of light — through Pakistani-Urdu tanveer from Arabic تَنْوِير (tanwir), the verbal noun of nawwara (to make luminous, to illuminate). The Quran uses the same root in some of its most luminous passages, including the Verse of Light (Ayat al-Nur, 24:35): 'Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth — the parable of His light is as a niche, within which is a lamp...' — the entire passage hinges on the verb nawwara.

In classical Islamic terminology tanwir denotes the act of bringing light: the lamp lit at sunset, the heart lit by knowledge, the mind lit by guidance. Sufi tradition speaks of three illuminations — tanwir al-batin (illumination of the inner self), tanwir al-aql (illumination of the intellect), and tanwir al-qalb (illumination of the heart). The 18th-century Mujaddid Shah Wali-Allah of Delhi used tanwir repeatedly in his philosophical works to describe how revelation makes the world intelligible.

Used as both a male and female name across the Pakistani-Urdu world; it is somewhat unusual in being more often female than male in Pakistani usage. Top Pakistani-Urdu name across the 1970s through 2000s. Variants: Tanvir, Tanveer, Tanwir, Tanwer. The Urdu/Persian rendering is تنویر.
