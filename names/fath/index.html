Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Fath

ف-ت-ح — The root f-t-h means to open, unlock, conquer, grant. Fath denotes the opening, the victory, the divine bestowal, and is the title of the 48th Surah of the Qur'an.

Dictionary Meaning

What Fath means

Opening, victory — from root ف-ت-ح (fataha — to open, to give victory). فتح (Fath — the opening, the victory). Surah Al-Fath (Chapter 48): 'Inna fatahna laka fathan mubina — Indeed We have given you a clear opening/victory' (48:1). This surah was revealed after the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah — which appeared to be a setback but Allah called it a clear fath (opening).

Fatih is the Turkish form — Sultan Mehmed II (Fatih Sultan Mehmed) who conquered Constantinople in 1453 bore this name. Fath names the man whose presence and effort opens what was closed.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Fath reveals

Fa is the letter of opening, Ta-light is the clear witness, and Ha-breath is the warm life-breath. The three letters together name a witnessed opening exhaled as living victory.

People often notice an opening-of-speech quality that gets the conversation moving in those who carry this name.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run discerning and clear-eyed in first impressions. The Ta that runs through the name points to a sense of completion and follow-through — someone who finishes what they start with the people they commit to. The closing Ha suggests a person who keeps life breathing forward, with few hard endings.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

The Portrait

What Fath asks of its bearer

People named Fath carry a quality of spiritually-opened, manifest, divine victory — they open what was closed. The Fa opens. The Ta manifests. The Ha breathes presence.

A Notable Bearer
