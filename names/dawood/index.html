Dictionary Meaning

What Dawood means

Beloved, the dearly loved one — from Hebrew Dawid (beloved), which passed into Arabic as داود (Dawood). Dawood (AS) is one of the great prophets of Islam — the Prophet-King who was given both prophethood and sovereignty, who slew Jalut (Goliath) as a young man, who received the Zabur (Psalms) as revelation, and whose voice was so beautiful that the birds and mountains joined his worship.

The Quran says: 'And We gave Dawood the Zabur' (17:55). The Prophet ﷺ said the best of fasting is the fast of Dawood — half the year. And of prayer, the prayer of Dawood — sleeping half the night, praying a third, sleeping a sixth. The name carries the full quality of the beloved: one who is chosen, gifted, and whose worship transforms the world around him.

The Letters

Reading Dawood through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior life. The final letter governs how things resolve.

دDalFirst · Outward SelfاAlifInteriorوWawInteriorدDalLast · Resolution

Dawood opens with Dal (د) — the door, return to origin, the guide who opens the way. Then Alif (ا) — origin, the first principle. Then Waw (و) — connection, the divine conjunction. Then Dal again (د) — the returning door completed. The name is framed by two Dals: door — origin — connection — door. Dawood begins and ends at the door of return. At the centre: Alif and Waw — origin connected. The beloved prophet: always at the door, connecting origin to what is beyond it, and returning.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Dawood asks of its bearer

People named Dawood tend to have a quality of being naturally beloved — others are drawn to them before they have done anything remarkable. The two Dals frame their life in return and guidance: they keep coming back to what matters, and they open doors for others. The Alif grounds them in origin. The Waw connects: they are the bridge. Their fullest expression is the person whose very presence is an act of worship.

A Notable Bearer

Dawood (AS) — Prophet and King, recipient of the Zabur (Psalms), the man who slew Jalut with a single stone. The Prophet ﷺ said: 'The most beloved of names to Allah includes Dawood.' The Quran calls him khalifah — vicegerent — on earth.
