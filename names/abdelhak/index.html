Dictionary Meaning

What Abdelhak means

Servant of the Truth — Abd + Al-Haqq (the Truth). Al-Haqq is a divine name: 'And you will know the truth (al-haqq)' (10:108). Abdelhak is the North African/French dominant spelling capturing Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia. Abdulhaq is the Pakistani/Gulf form. Two distinct search communities, same divine quality.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Abdelhak

ح-ق-ق — From ḥ-q-q, truth, reality, what is rightly due — al-Ḥaqq (the Truth, the Real) is the divine name in ʿAbd al-Ḥaqq.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Abdelhak reveals

Ain-Ba-Dal of the servant, Ha-breath the breath of life, Qaf the reaching of the heart toward nearness. Servant, breath, nearness — one whose every breath is bent toward the Real.

The Portrait

What Abdelhak asks of its bearer

The first letter of Abdelhak is Ain — an outward self that sees deeply. Inside the name, Ba brings an interior that holds and shelters; Dal brings an interior of return. These are the hidden weathers of the name. The Qaf resolves the name: the life resolves in standing power. What this asks of him: to let servant of Truth pass through this whole sequence — opening, interior, close — until it is no longer a label but a way of being.
