What Waqas means

From the Arabic waqqāṣ (وقاص) — 'the breaker', from the root w-q-ṣ meaning to break the neck of an enemy in battle. The most weight-bearing bearer is Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ (d. 674 CE) — one of the ten Companions promised paradise (al-ʿasharah al-mubashsharah), the conqueror of Persia, and an uncle of the Prophet ﷺ on his mother's side. The Prophet said of him: 'This is my uncle; let any man show me his uncle!' (Tirmidhi). Saʿd led the Muslim armies at the decisive battle of Qadisiyyah in 636 CE.

To name a son Waqas (or with the patronymic Saʿd Waqas) in Pakistani, Indian, and Bangladeshi Muslim families is a deliberate honour to the Companion. Top-tier boys' name in Pakistan from the 1970s onward.

Variants: Waqas, Waqqas, Waqaas, Wakas, Wagas. Pair-names in Pakistani families: Bilal, Salman, Hamza, Saad. The full traditional form is Waqqaṣ ibn Abī Waqqāṣ.

Reading Waqas through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

وWawFirst · Outward SelfقQafInteriorاAlifInteriorصSadLast · Resolution

Waw (connection) — Qaf (divine axis) — Alif (origin) — Sad (sincere truth). Sharp: connecting, absolute, originary, sincere.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."
