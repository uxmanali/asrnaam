Dictionary Meaning

What Ibn Bajjah means

Son of Bajja, variously read as 'silversmith' (al-Ṣāʾigh) or after the family's place. From the family name باجَّة (Bājja), of disputed origin; the title al-Ṣāʾigh ('the goldsmith') is the more common attribution.

Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Ibn Bajjah.

  • Abū Bakr Muḥammad ibn Yaḥyā ibn al-Ṣāʾigh ibn Bājja (c. 1085–1138 CE · Zaragoza / Granada / Fez). Known to Latin Europe as Avempace, Ibn Bājja opened the Andalusian philosophical tradition that would culminate in Ibn Ṭufayl and Ibn Rushd. His Tadbīr al-Mutawaḥḥid (Governance of the Solitary) sets out a theory of the philosopher who must withdraw from a corrupted city. He also wrote commentaries on Aristotle's physics and produced significant work on music theory and pharmacology.

Legacy

What Ibn Bajjah left behind

Ibn Bājja's image of the philosopher in voluntary solitude, the mutawaḥḥid, became the template for Ibn Ṭufayl's Ḥayy and a reference point for later European thought on the relationship between the individual mind and civic life.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
