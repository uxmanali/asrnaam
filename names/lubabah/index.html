Dictionary Meaning

What Lubabah means

Essence — deeper content page for Lubabah. Lubabah bint al-Harith al-Hilaliyya (RA) — also called Lubabah al-Kubra — was one of the very first women to accept Islam, accepting alongside Khadijah (RA). She was the wife of Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib (the Prophet's uncle) and mother of Ibn Abbas (RA) — the greatest interpreter of the Quran among the companions.

How This Shows Up in Life

Personality & relational pattern

People named Lubabah tend to be drawing others in through warmth and attachment. The Ba that runs through the name points to a steady, grounded inner posture that values structure — someone who builds durable relationships from a foundation of service. The closing Ta suggests a person who completes things cleanly, with few loose ends.

An interpretive reading drawn from the letters of the name — for reflection, not prediction.

The Letters

Reading Lubabah through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

لLamFirst · Outward SelfauthorityبBaInteriorvesselاAlifInteriororiginبBaInteriorvesselةTaLast · Resolutionmanifestation

Lam gives authority, Ba holds, Alif grounds, Ba holds again, Ta manifests. Essence: authoritative, doubly holding, originary, manifest.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi

The Portrait

What Lubabah asks of its bearer

Lubabah opens with Lam — an outward self that binds and attaches. At the interior, Ba brings an interior that holds and shelters; Alif brings an originary axis at the interior. These are what the name does without ever announcing it. The closing letter is Alif: everything resolves back to the source. The bearer is asked to inhabit each letter consciously, until deeper content is not what the name says but what the life shows.

A Notable Bearer

Mother of Ibn Abbas (RA) — 'O Allah, teach him the interpretation of the Quran' — the Prophet's prayer that made Ibn Abbas the greatest Quranic commentator among companions.

About the name Lubabah

What does the name Lubabah mean?
Lubabah means: Deeper content — Lubaba bint al-Harith, one of the first Muslims. One of the first Muslim women. Wife of Abbas (Prophet's uncle). Mother of Ibn Abbas (RA) — Tarjuman al-Quran. Deeper content page.
Is Lubabah mentioned in the Quran?
Ibn Abbas (RA) — Lubabah's son — is called Tarjuman al-Quran (Interpreter of the Quran). The Prophet prayed: 'O Allah, teach him the interpretation of the Quran.' Lubabah raised this great scholar.

Is Lubabah a good Islamic name?
Yes, Lubabah is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: Deeper content — Lubaba bint al-Harith, one of the first Muslims. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
