Dictionary Meaning

What Kerem means

The Turkish form of the Arabic karam (كرم) — generosity, nobility of character, the giving without counting. Al-Karīm (الكريم) — 'the Most Generous' — is one of Allah's ninety-nine names, mentioned in the Quran's invocation: rabbī al-karīm 'my Lord, the Generous' (27:40). The Quran itself is called al-Qurʾān al-karīm — the noble Quran.

A consistently top-tier Turkish boys' name through the modern era. Kerem also carries the weight of Anatolian folk literature: the romance of Kerem ile Aslı is one of the great Turkish-Azeri love stories, telling of a young man whose love is tested through fire and patience — a name that for generations of Turkish parents has meant noble heart.

Bearer notes: Kerem Bürsin, Turkish actor; Kerem Aktürkoğlu, footballer. Variants: Karem, Karim (Arabic), Kareem (Anglo). Related: Karam, Akram, Kerim, Kareema (f).
