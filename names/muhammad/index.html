Dictionary Meaning

What Muhammad means

The most praised, the most praiseworthy — from root ح-م-د (hamida — to praise). محمد (Muhammad — the one who is repeatedly and greatly praised). The name appears twice in the Quran: 'Muhammad is not but a messenger' (3:144) and 'Muhammad is the messenger of Allah' (48:29). He is also named Ahmad (the most praiseful, the one who praises most) in the Quran (61:6).

The most common name in the world — borne by hundreds of millions across every continent. The Prophet ﷺ said: 'On the Day of Judgment, you will be called by your names and your fathers' names, so make your names beautiful.' He is the one whose name must be followed by the prayer of blessing: sallallahu alayhi wa sallam.

The Letters

Reading Muhammad through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

مMeemFirst · Outward SelfحHaInteriorمMeemInteriorدDalLast · Resolution

Muhammad opens with Meem (م) — love, depth, the sustaining. Then Ha (ح) — the breath of life. Then Meem (م) — love again. Then Dal (د) — the returning door. Love — breath of life — love — the returning door. The most praised: beginning and returning in love, animated by the breath of life, always opening the returning door. The Prophet's quality is love, twice, with life between them, and the door always open.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi

The Portrait

What Muhammad asks of its bearer

People named Muhammad carry the weight and aspiration of the greatest human who ever lived. The double Meem gives love at both ends. The Ha breathes life between them. The Dal close is the returning door: they are always returning to what their name demands of them — the aspiration to the most praised quality of character.

A Notable Bearer

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ — the seal of the prophets, the mercy to all worlds, the most praised of all creation. 'And We have not sent you except as a mercy to the worlds' — Quran 21:107.
