Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Ibn Kathir's letters draw from many elements at once, a full palette, not a single note. This is a name that asks its bearer to hold contradictions gracefully: warmth and steadiness, movement and depth. Running through the reading is Al-Ahad, the One, carried by a person as a singular, undivided presence. A person named Ibn Kathir is many things at once, and gets to choose which shows up when.

Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Abū al-Fidāʾ ʿImād al-Dīn Ismāʿīl ibn ʿUmar ibn Kathīr al-Dimashqī (c. 1300–1373 CE · Buṣrā (Syria) → Damascus). Al-Ḥāfiẓ Ibn Kathīr was a leading scholar of fourteenth-century Damascus and a close student of Ibn Taymiyya. His Tafsīr al-Qurʾān al-ʿAẓīm, known simply as Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr, is one of the most widely read Sunni Qurʾān commentaries, distinguished by its method of explaining the Qurʾān first by the Qurʾān itself, then by hadith, then by the statements of the Companions and the early generations. His al-Bidāya wa-l-Nihāya (The Beginning and the End) is a major historical work running from creation through the events of his own lifetime, particularly valuable for the history of the later Mamlūk period.
