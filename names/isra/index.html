Dictionary Meaning

What Isra means

The Night Journey — Surah Al-Isra (Chapter 17). 'Glory be to the One who took His servant by night from Masjid al-Haram to Masjid al-Aqsa' (17:1). The night of the Prophet's journey from Makkah to Jerusalem and then to the heavens (Miraj). Isra names the woman who carries the quality of this miraculous night journey — the quality of being carried by divine grace to the highest stations in the darkness.

The Letters

Reading Isra through Ilm ul Huroof

Every Arabic name is a sequence of ontological principles. The first letter governs the outward self. The middle letters govern the interior. The final letter governs how things resolve.

إAlifFirst · Outward SelforiginسSinInteriorexpansionرRaInteriormercyاAlifInteriororiginءHamzaLast · Resolutionpure being

Alif (ا) — origin. Sin (س) — flowing expansion. Ra (ر) — perpetual mercy. Alif (ا) — return. Hamza (ء) — strong beginning. Night journey: originary, expanding through the night, merciful, returning, strongly beginning at the highest station.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

In the tradition of Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi

The Portrait

What Isra asks of its bearer

The reading of Isra begins with Alif: primary orientation toward being first. Beneath the surface, Seen brings an interior of asking; Ra brings an interior of repetition and momentum. These are the inward currents the name carries. And Isra closes on Alif: everything resolves back to the source. What this asks of him: to let the Night Journey pass through this whole sequence — opening, interior, close — until it is no longer a label but a way of being.

About the name Isra

What does the name Isra mean?
Isra means: The Night Journey — Surah 17, the Prophet's ascension from Masjid al-Aqsa. Surah Al-Isra (Chapter 17): 'Glory be to the One who took His servant by night' (17:1). The night journey from Makkah to Al-Aqsa to the heavens.
Is Isra mentioned in the Quran?
Yes — Surah Al-Isra (Chapter 17): 'Subhana alladhi asra bi-abdihi laylan min al-Masjid al-Haram ila al-Masjid al-Aqsa' (17:1). Directly Quranic.

Is Isra a good Islamic name?
Yes, Isra is an excellent Islamic name. It carries the meaning: The Night Journey — Surah 17, the Prophet's ascension from Masjid al-Aqsa. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will be called by your names on the Day of Resurrection, so give yourselves good names" (Abu Dawud).
