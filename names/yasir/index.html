Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Yasir

ي-س-ر — from y-s-r, to be easy. Yāsir is the one for whom things are made easy; same root as taysīr (facilitation). ʿAmmār ibn Yāsir was a Companion of the Prophet ﷺ, son of Yāsir and Sumayya (the first martyr).

Dictionary Meaning

What Yasir means

The easy-going, the one who chooses ease — from root y-s-r (yasira — to be easy, to choose the easy path). Yasir ibn Amir — the father of Ammar and husband of Sumayyah, who became one of the first martyrs of Islam. The Prophet passed by while the family was being tortured and said: 'Patience, O family of Yasir — your meeting place is paradise.'.

Yasser Arafat bore this name. Top Pakistani and Arab boys name.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Yasir reveals

Ya of reaching opens, Alif of origin, Sin of secret, Ya, Ra of mercy. Easiness reaching mercy.

People often notice a particularity in how they care for this person, this moment in those who carry this name.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run reaching and aspirational in how they meet the world. The Alif that runs through the name points to a strong sense of self and a tendency to begin things — someone who draws others toward them as a starting point. The closing Ra suggests a person who lets endings push into the next beginning.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

The Portrait

What Yasir asks of its bearer

The reading of Yasir begins with Ya: an outward self of strong personality. Through the interior, Alif brings an originary axis at the interior; Seen brings an interior of asking. These are what the name does without ever announcing it. The closing letter is Ra: the life resolves through motion. What this asks of him: to let ease, the one who makes things easy pass through this whole sequence — opening, interior, close — until it is no longer a label but a way of being.
