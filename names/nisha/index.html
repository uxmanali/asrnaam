Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

Multiple origins. In Arabic, related to nashʾa (rising, growth). In Hindi, nishā means night. South Asian Muslim girls' name.

Nun of depth opens, Ya of reaching, Sheen of radiance, Alif of return. A reaching radiance returning to the source.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Nisha joins fire to earth, the will that decides with the patience that lasts. This is a name that asks its bearer to burn steadily rather than in bursts, and to plant what the fire has cleared. Running through the reading is Al-Nur, the Light, carried as a light that steadies the room, and beside it Al-Ahad, the One, adding a singular, undivided presence. A person named Nisha tends to build things that outlast them.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

Night, from Sanskrit/Hindi-Urdu nisha (night). Used by South Asian Muslim girls. Pakistani, Indian Muslim, Bangladeshi communities who use Urdu-Sanskrit hybrid naming. Also close to Arabic nisa (women, the plural of woman) which appears in Surah Al-Nisa (Chapter 4, Women). The night in Islamic tradition is a sacred time, 'Arise and pray at night except for a little' (73:2).
