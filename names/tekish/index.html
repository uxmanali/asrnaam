Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

Old Turkic tekiš, "one who strikes / contender." A Khwarezmian Turkic regnal name.

A Turkic war-name in the Khwarezmian house, marking the conqueror who established imperial sway over Iran.

Read through the Science of Letters, Tekish opens with Ta (ت), the letter of completion, and resolves on Sheen (ش), the letter of expansion. Between the two stand origin, covering, origin, expansion., a chord of interior qualities the name carries forward.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Tekish's letters draw from many elements at once, a full palette, not a single note. This is a name that asks its bearer to hold contradictions gracefully: warmth and steadiness, movement and depth. Running through the reading is Al-Tawwab, the Relenting, carried by a person as the readiness to return and be returned to. A person named Tekish is many things at once, and gets to choose which shows up when.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

The striker, the contender, the Khwarezmshah who took over from the late Seljuks in northern Iran.

Section 5 · Bearers

Who Has Carried This Name

  • Ala ad-Din Tekish (d. 1200 CE · Khwarezm). Khwarezmshah; consolidated Khwarezmian rule over Iran after defeating the last Seljuk sultan Tughril III.
