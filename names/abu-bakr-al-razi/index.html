Famous Bearer

The historical figure who carried the name Abu Bakr al-Razi.

  • Abū Bakr Muḥammad ibn Zakariyyāʾ al-Rāzī (c. 854 – c. 925 CE · Rayy / Baghdad). Abū Bakr al-Rāzī directed hospitals in Rayy and Baghdad. His Kitāb al-Ḥāwī (Liber Continens in Latin) is a vast clinical compilation of his own case notes alongside Greek, Indian, and Persian sources. His Kitāb al-Manṣūrī provided a more compact medical reference. His treatise on smallpox and measles, al-Judarī wa-l-Ḥaṣba, is the first known systematic clinical distinction between the two diseases. He defended philosophical positions, including the rejection of prophecy, that placed him outside Islamic theological mainstream, making him a controversial figure even in his lifetime.

Legacy

What Abu Bakr al-Razi left behind

The Ḥāwī was translated into Latin in the late thirteenth century and printed throughout the Renaissance. His clinical descriptions retained authority in European medicine until the eighteenth century. He is widely cited as a founding figure of evidence-based diagnosis in medical history.

Scholarly assessment of a figure of this scale remains an active conversation; this entry summarises the dominant historical reading.
