Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

ج-ه-د

ج-ه-د, from j-h-d, to strive. Mujāhid is the striver, the one who exerts effort in the way of God. Q 4:95 distinguishes the mujāhidūn from the sitters.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

The letters in Mujahid cluster around fire, the element of will, warmth, and forward motion. This is a name that leans toward decisiveness and radiance, with the courage to begin what others hesitate to. Threaded through it is Al-Mu'min, the Faithful, carried by a person as a faith others can lean on. A person named Mujahid is likely to warm the rooms they enter and to lead by lit example.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

The striver, the one who exerts total effort, from root ج-ه-د. مجاهد (mujahid, the one who strives, the active striver). The Quran honours the mujahideen: 'Allah has preferred the mujahideen over those who sit still by a great reward' (4:95). The striving encompasses every domain, against the lower self, against injustice, in every form of good effort.

Mujahid ibn Jabr (642–722 CE) was one of the greatest Quran commentators (mufassireen) of the tabi'in generation, his tafsir is among the oldest and most authoritative.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the name Mujahid mean?

Mujahid means: The striver, the one who gives his total effort in the path of God. In Islamic tradition, this name carries deep significance rooted in Arabic etymology and spiritual heritage.

Is Mujahid a good Islamic name?

Yes, Mujahid is an excellent Islamic name. The Prophet ﷺ encouraged giving children beautiful names with good meanings. Mujahid carries the striver, the one who gives his total effort in the path of god, making it a blessed and meaningful choice.

Is Mujahid mentioned in the Quran?

No. Mujahid is not directly mentioned in the Quran by name, but the name carries deep Islamic meaning and is widely used in Muslim communities.

What is the spiritual meaning of the name Mujahid?

In Ilm ul Huroof (the Islamic Science of Letters), Mujahid opens with Meem (م), love. Each letter carries an ontological principle that shapes the character of the person who bears this name.
