Dictionary Meaning

What Mujahid means

The striver, the one who exerts total effort — from root ج-ه-د. مجاهد (mujahid — the one who strives, the active striver). The Quran honours the mujahideen: 'Allah has preferred the mujahideen over those who sit still by a great reward' (4:95). The striving encompasses every domain — against the lower self, against injustice, in every form of good effort.

Mujahid ibn Jabr (642–722 CE) was one of the greatest Quran commentators (mufassireen) of the tabi'in generation — his tafsir is among the oldest and most authoritative.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Mujahid

ج-ه-د — from j-h-d, to strive. Mujāhid is the striver, the one who exerts effort in the way of God. Q 4:95 distinguishes the mujāhidūn from the sitters.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Mujahid reveals

Meem of love opens, Jeem of inner beauty, Alif of origin, Ha of presence, Dal of return. Striving made beauty, presence, return.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run soft and loving, instinctively familial in their bearing. The Jeem that runs through the name points to a hidden interior life that lives in the depth more than the surface — someone who stays private with their feelings and is slow to fully open up. The closing Dal suggests a person who leaves the door open — endings are rarely final.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

The Letters

Reading Mujahid through Ilm ul Huroof

Each Arabic letter in Mujahid sits in a position. The first opens onto the world, the middle letters hold the inner work, the last settles how things finish.

مMeemFirst · Outward SelfجJeemInteriorاAlifInteriorهHaInteriorدDalLast · Resolution

Mujahid opens with Meem (م) — love. Then Jeem (ج) — comprehensive beauty. Then Alif (ا) — origin. Then Ha (ه) — divine presence. Then Dal (د) — returning door. Love — comprehensive beauty — origin — divine presence — returning door. The striver: grounded in love, gathering beauty through effort, rooted in origin, animated by divine presence, always returning through the door.

The Portrait

What Mujahid asks of its bearer

People named Mujahid tend to commit totally to what they believe in. The Meem grounds in love: their striving is loving. The Jeem gathers beautifully. The Ha breathes divine presence. The Dal close keeps returning: their effort always opens something.

A Notable Bearer

Mujahid ibn Jabr (642–722 CE) — the great Quran commentator who reportedly studied the entire Quran with Ibn Abbas (RA) over 30 times. His tafsir is the earliest authoritative commentary on the Quran.
