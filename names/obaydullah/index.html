Dictionary Meaning

What Obaydullah means

Little servant of Allah — from عبيد (ubayd — small servant, diminutive of abd) + الله (Allah). The diminutive form expresses not smallness of status but depth of humility — the person who considers themselves the smallest, most humble servant of Allah. This is the quality of the truly great: the greater the person, the more they experience themselves as small before the divine.

Ubaydullah ibn Jahsh (RA) and many companions bore this name. It is used widely across the Arab world and South Asia as a name of deep piety and humble devotion.

Arabic Root Meaning

Root of Obaydullah

ع-ب-د + ا-ل-ل-ه — Arabic compound ʿUbaydullāh, 'little servant of God'. ʿUbaydullāh ibn ʿAbbās (RA) was a famed early Muslim.

Ilm ul Haroof Meaning

What Obaydullah reveals

Ain of perception, Ba of foundation, Ya of reaching, Dal of return — then the Name of Allah. A small servanthood given the highest possession.

People often notice a steady attachment in love and loyalty, slow to leave anything in those who carry this name.

The Reading

Personality & relational pattern

Bearers of this name often run perceptive and watchful in how they meet people. The Lam that runs through the name points to an interior shaped by attachment — to people, to faith, to place — someone who lets love become the through-line of their relationships. The closing Ha suggests a person who keeps life breathing forward, with few hard endings.

Read this as a mirror, not a verdict.

The Letters

Reading Obaydullah through Ilm ul Huroof

Obaydullah reads as a sequence. The opening letter (Ain) sets how the name meets the world; the middle shapes interior life; the closing letter governs how matters resolve.

عAinFirst · Outward SelfبBaInteriorيYaInteriorدDalInteriorاAlifInteriorلLamInteriorلLamInteriorهHaLast · Resolution

Obaydullah opens with Ain (ع) — the perceiving spring. Then Ba (ب) — the vessel. Then Ya (ي) — reaching. Then Dal (د) — returning door. Then the divine name Allah: Alif-Lam-Lam-Ha — origin, divine authority (twice), divine presence. The perceiving spring — vessel — reaching — returning door — then into the divine name. The humble servant: perceiving from the spring, becoming a vessel, reaching, returning, entering the divine name entirely.

"A name is not a label assigned after the fact. It is a structure of principles that begins its work the moment it is given."

The Portrait

What Obaydullah asks of its bearer

People named Obaydullah tend to have a quality of genuine humility that others find both rare and inspiring. The Ain gives them clear perception. The Ba makes them a vessel — they contain without claiming. The Dal keeps returning to servitude. The entry into the divine name means their entire quality is in service of the divine: the most authentic form of servitude.
