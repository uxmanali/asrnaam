Section 1 · Arabic Root

Where the Name Comes From

Arabic prophetic name Yūsuf (Joseph) son of Tāshfīn, a Berber Sanhaja name.

Yūsuf is a prophetic name; Tāshfīn comes from the Berber Sanhaja confederation that gave rise to the Almoravid movement.

Section 3 · Synthesis

What This Reading Says About You

Yusuf ibn Tashfin joins fire to earth, the will that decides with the patience that lasts. This is a name that asks its bearer to burn steadily rather than in bursts, and to plant what the fire has cleared. Running through the reading is Al-Qayyum, the Self-Sustaining, carried as a self-holding constancy, and beside it Al-Nur, the Light, adding a light that steadies the room. A person named Yusuf ibn Tashfin tends to build things that outlast them.

Section 4 · What Families Say

What People Usually Mean by This Name

Yusuf, son of Tashfin: Yusuf ibn Tashfin (r. 1061 to 1106), the Almoravid amir al-muslimin who unified al-Andalus and the Maghreb at the end of the eleventh century.

Called from Marrakesh to al-Andalus after the fall of Toledo (1085), Yusuf ibn Tashfin defeated the Castilians at the Battle of Zallaqa (1086), stabilised Muslim Spain, and refused the title of caliph out of humility, keeping the classical style amir al-muslimin.

The name preserves the memory of one of the great restorers of Muslim political unity across the western Mediterranean.
