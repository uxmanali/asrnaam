Dictionary Meaning

What Fahd means

Fahd (فهد) is the Arabic word for the leopard, specifically the cheetah-like fahd of the Arabian and North African deserts. Classical Arabic distinguished fahd (the smaller spotted hunter, often tamed for falconry-like coursing of gazelles) from namir (the larger leopard) and asad (the lion). Pre-Islamic and early Islamic literature is full of references to the fahd as a hunting companion of Bedouin princes and as a symbol of swift, decisive action.

The name has been one of the most common masculine names in Saudi Arabia, the Gulf, and the wider Arabian Peninsula for centuries. Its most internationally recognised bearer is King Fahd ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz (1921–2005), the fifth king of Saudi Arabia, who took the title Khādim al-Ḥaramayn ash-Sharīfayn (Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques). The name is also widespread across Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, and the UAE, often paired with the patronymic ibn followed by the father's name. Notable younger bearers include the footballer Fahd al-Muwallad and the singer Fahd Bilal.
